               <a href="">
                <b>
                 Get to know the Class of 2020
                </b>
               </a>
               <br />
               Muhlenberg's newest students bring a wealth of experiences to campus
               <br />
               <a href="">
               </a>
               <p>
                <b>
                </b>
                <br />
                Fri, 02 Sep 2016 16:34:00 EDT
               </p>
               <p>
                The heat of pre-season is over for student-athletes. Pre-Orientation experiences left new students with great connections. The lines of cars loaded with belongings are long gone. First-Year Orientation Weekend is now just a happy memory.
               </p>
               <p>
                For the Class of 2020, Muhlenberg is beginning to feel like home.
               </p>
               <p>
                At the opening of the fall semester, the Muhlenberg community grew by 593 first-year students. Just like classes before them, the Class of 2020 brings with them diverse backgrounds and experiences that will continue to enhance the richness of the Muhlenberg experience.
               </p>
               <p>
                This is the second most ethnically and internationally diverse class in the history of the College, with 18.2% of students ethnically and internationally diverse.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/admissions/ClassDiversity-460x276.png" alt="Class of 2020 - Diversity graphic" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " />
               </p>
               <p>
                The class also boasts an all-time record for geographical diversity, with 30% from New Jersey; 22% from Pennsylvania; 18% from New York; 9% from New England; 7% from Maryland, Virginia, the District of Columbia and Delaware; 5% from the western part of the U.S.; 4% from outside the U.S.; 3% from the Midwest; and 2% from the southern part of the U.S.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/admissions/Geo-460x284.png" alt="Class of 2020 - geographic distribution graphic" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " />
               </p>
               <p>
                The Class of 2020 has already excelled in the classroom, with 36% of them graduating in the top tenth of their high school classes. They will undoubtedly continue to excel academically during their time on campus.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/admissions/Top10-1-460x284.png" alt="Class of 2020 - top 10% graphic" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " />
               </p>
               <p>
                <img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/admissions/Majors-229x317.png" alt="Class of 2020 - majors graphic" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />
                While a large part of the Class of 2020 has yet to determine a potential major, the top degrees they are interested in include theatre, biology, business, psychology and neuroscience.
               </p>
               <p>
                Members of the Class of 2020 have already joined their peers in making their marks outside the wall of a classroom.
               </p>
               <p>
                In the arts sphere, one first-year co-wrote and acted in two short films that both received &ldquo;Best of Boston&rdquo; awards at the Boston 48-Hour Film Festival, while a classmate is a member of &ldquo;Surge,&rdquo; a hip-hop dance team that has performed with the Harlem Globetrotters, the Philadelphia Wings and others.
               </p>
               <p>
                When it comes to the sciences, a member of the Class of 2020 has served as president of the Model World Health Organization, which has received thanks from the Ambassador from Kenya for their initiatives in Africa.
               </p>
               <p>
                The incoming class has also excelled in media-related endeavors. One student is director and producer of a hometown television talk show with a focus on local, national and international political issues. Another has written and edited pieces for Ireland&rsquo;s largest online newspaper.
               </p>
               <p>
                Unsurprisingly, a number of first-year students have been heavily involved in social justice-related projects, a tradition that will surely continue and grow at Muhlenberg. One student is the founder of The Validation Project&mdash;now a worldwide organization dedicated to stopping bullying and building self-esteem among young people.&nbsp; Another co-founded the iTold4 Project, which has now educated over 2,300 students on how to prevent hearing loss.
               </p>
               <p>
                In J.K Rowling&rsquo;s Harry Potter books, a series this class grew up reading, wand maker Mr. Ollivander tells the titular character &ldquo;I think it is clear that we can expect great things from you.&rdquo; The same can be said of the Class of 2020&mdash;we look forward to seeing all that you accomplish. Welcome to Muhlenberg.
               </p>
               <p>
                &nbsp;
               </p>
              </td>
