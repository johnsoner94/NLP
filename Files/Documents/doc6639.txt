           <p>
            <strong>
             November 19-21, 2010
            </strong>
            -
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/spanishfaculty/mirnatrauger/">
             Mirna Trauger
            </a>

            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/spanishfaculty/flormariabuitrago/">

            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/staff/helenbachochin/">
             Helen Bachochin

            and  (all lecturers in the Spanish department) attended the American Council of Teaching Foreign Languages conference in Boston.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             November 12, 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/lisaperfetti/">
             Lisa Perfetti
            </a>
            , associate professor of French, was elected to the advisory board of the Society for Medieval Feminist Scholarship.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             November 3-7, 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/lisaperfetti/">
             Lisa Perfetti
            </a>
            (French), Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/kathrynwixon/">
             Kathy Wixon
            </a>
            (French), Trish Boyles (ABE), Jane Flood (Physics), and Kathy Harring (Psychology) attended the 35th annual Professional and Organizational Development Network in Higher Education conference in St. Louis. Harring and
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/lisaperfetti/">
             Perfetti
            </a>
            presented an interactive session titled
            <em>
             "Controversial Conversations: Engaging Students and Faculty."
            </em>
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             November 5, 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/eileenmcewan/">
             Eileen McEwan
            </a>
            , associate professor of French, attended the American Council for Quebec Studies conference in Burlington, VT, where she chaired a panel and presented a paper entitled
            <em>
             "Endurer ou durer? A new feminine 'survivance' in Franco-American and Quebecois literature."
            </em>
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             November 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/germanfaculty/albertkipa/">
             Albert Kipa
            </a>
            , Professor Laureate of German and Russian, was named the 2010 post-secondary level Educator of the Year by The Pennsylvania State Modern Language Association (PSMLA). The award plaque, presented at the PSMLA's Fall Conference in Erie, Pa., cites "distinguished teaching and professional contributions to World languages and cultures."
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             October 2010
            </strong>
            -
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/spanishfaculty/flormariabuitrago/">
             Flor Maria Buitrago
            </a>
            , lecturer in Spanish, was invited to give a lecture to Computer Aid, Inc., entitled
            <em>
             "Colombia: the Land of Coffee, Emerald and Flowers."
            </em>
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             October 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/germanfaculty/albertkipa/">
             Albert Kipa
            </a>
            , Professor Laureate of German and Russian, and Saeger Professor of Comparative Literature, is co-translator and co-editor of
            <em>
             The Lay of Ihor&rsquo;s Campaign
            </em>
            , the greatest literary monument of Kyivan (Kievan) Rus&rsquo;. The vast majority of scholars today accept
            <em>
             The Lay
            </em>
            as a work of the 12th century, although it was first deciphered, edited and published in St. Petersburg in 1800. The new translation is printed in
            <em>
             The Ukrainian Quarterly
            </em>
            (Vol. LXV, No. 4, Winter 2009, pp. 293-323) to mark the 210th anniversary of the work&rsquo;s first appearance.&nbsp;
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             September 30 - October 2, 2010
            </strong>
            - Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/spanishfaculty/erikasutherland/">
             Erika M. Sutherland
            </a>
            , associate professor of Spanish, presented her paper
            <em>
             "El texto trampol&iacute;n: Saltos y juegos malabares para principiantes [The text as trampoline: Backflips and tricks for beginners]"
            </em>
            at the ASELE conference in Salamanca, Spain. The paper presents a methodology for incorporating advanced texts in elementary-level language classes.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             October 1-2, 2010
            </strong>
            - Eleven Muhlenberg faculty and administrators attended&nbsp;the Connections Across the Curriculum: Integration and Assessment conference held at Moravian College, October 1-2. The conference was supported by a collaborative Teagle Foundation grant awarded to Moravian, Muhlenberg, Drew, Roanoke and Susquehanna, and organized by Curt Keim (Moravian) and
            <em>
             Kathy Harring
            </em>
            (Muhlenberg).&nbsp; Participants included
            <em>
             Trish Boyles, Keri Colabroy, Cathy Ouellette
            </em>
            ,
            <em>
             Pearl Rosenberg and John Ramsay
            </em>
            .
            <em>
             Kathy Harring
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             Laura Edelman
            </em>
            presented a session titled
            <em>
             "Brain-Based Pedagogy for Integrative Learning."
            </em>
            Dr.
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/frenchfaculty/lisaperfetti/">
             Lisa Perfetti
            </a>
            ,
            <em>
             Jim Bloom, Jen Jarson
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             Bruce Wightman
            </em>
