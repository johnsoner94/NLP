          <h3>
           Student-Faculty Collaboration
          </h3>
          <p>
           In almost all of our activities, we find ways for motivated students to be involved. This kind of collaboration happens most frequently with respect to
           <a href="/main/academics/psychology/research/">
            research
           </a>
           . Specific faculty research interests vary widely (e.g., understanding trauma, risk perception, perceptions of ADHD and autism, music perception, multiculturalism, LGBTQ issues in Black populations). See our
           <a href="/main/academics/psychology/faculty/">
            faculty pages
           </a>
           for more information about current research interests. Dozens of students each semester work directly with faculty on their research, and develop independent projects in collaboration with faculty. Each year, additional students choose to participate in our
           <a href="/main/academics/psychology/seniorthesis/">
            Thesis Program
           </a>
           to work even more independently, with faculty mentoring. Two of those thesis students are chosen as recipients of each of the two departmental annual research awards: The Rosenberg Award and the Crist Family Research Fellowship.
