          <div style="margin-right: 150px;">
           <table cellpadding="12" border="0">
            <tr valign="top">
             <td>
              <img src="../../../../images/main/academics/theatre-dance/headshotlarge_005.jpg" width="120" height="144" alt="Kelly Howe" />
             </td>
             <td>
              <p style="font-weight: bold; font-size: 14px">
               <span style="font-size: 16px">
                Kelly Howe
                <br />
               </span>
               Class of 2003
               <br />
               Major in Theatre
              </p>
              <p style="font-size: 14px">
               Assistant Professor of Theatre, Director
              </p>
             </td>
            </tr>
           </table>
           <div style="line-height:140%; font-size:120%; margin-left:40px;">
            <p style="margin-bottom:0px;">
             <b>
              After graduation, I worked in the Education Department
             </b>
             at Actors Theatre of Louisville before earning my M.A./Ph.D. in performance
             <img src="../../../../images/main/academics/theatre-dance/currentwork.jpg" alt="Kelly Howe" width="272" height="227" align="right" style="margin-right:-100px;" />
             as public practice at the University of Texas at Austin. As an assistant professor at North Central College, near Chicago, I teach courses in theatre history, activist theatre, script analysis, acting, theatrical dialogue facilitation, and feminist and queer theory. As a scholar I specialize in activist performance, an emphasis reflected in my work as president of Pedagogy and Theatre of the Oppressed, Inc., an international organization of artists, activists, educators, scholars, and community organizers (
             <a href="http://www.ptoweb.org" target="_blank">
              www.ptoweb.org
             </a>
             ).
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom:0px;">
             <b>
              Muhlenberg&rsquo;s  sophisticated studio training
             </b>
             and rigorous liberal arts curriculum prepared me for a rich, fulfilling life in the arts. I discovered the teacher/artist/scholar/activist I wanted to become. Generous, brilliant mentors helped
             <img src="../../../../images/main/academics/theatre-dance/currentwork2.jpg" alt="Kelly Howe" width="250" height="212" align="left" style="margin-left:-35px; margin-right:12px; " />
             me build an academic/artistic profile that reflected my passions and appealed to graduate schools. Whether I&rsquo;m directing a play, mentoring advisees, or facilitating class discussion, I consistently witness my own students benefitting from skills I gained at Muhlenberg. Wherever I go, I&rsquo;m proud to claim Muhlenberg as my alma mater.
            </p>
