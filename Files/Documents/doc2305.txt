           <h3>
            ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCE (ESC)
           </h3>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Description of the major
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            A major in environmental science provides students with many opportunities in industry, government, and academia.&nbsp; For example, graduates are well prepared for careers in environmental consulting, environmental regulation, advocacy, or education.&nbsp; Students can also pursue graduate studies in a wide range of fields including environmental science, ecology, oceanography, forestry, law, medicine, and toxicology.
           </p>
           <p>
            Students are encouraged to obtain internship experience in specialized fields of Environmental Science and/or participate in an environmentally oriented study abroad program during their junior year.&nbsp; Recently, students have served with local educational institutions, nonprofit organizations, state and federal government, and local industries.&nbsp; Others have spent time in Australia, Ecuador, Costa Rica, Canada and other countries.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             MAJOR REQUIREMENTS
             <br />
            </strong>
            To be retained as a major, a student must maintain a 2.0 average based on all biology, chemistry, physics, mathematics, environmental science, and environmental studies courses attempted.
            <strong>
             &nbsp;
            </strong>
            Majors take the 9 required courses from list A and
            <strong>
             a total of 5 electives from lists B and C
            </strong>
            (see the course lists below).&nbsp; At least 3 elective courses must come from list B and at least 1 course must come from list C.&nbsp; The last elective can come from either list.&nbsp; Students interested in
            <a href="#teacherc">
             teacher certification
            </a>
            may also complete additional courses.
           </p>
           <p>
            A.

             CORE SCIENCE COURSES

            (10 courses)

           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>

           <p align="center">
            <strong>
             SUSTAINABILITY STUDIES (SUS)
             <br />
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Description of the minor (minor program only)
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Sustainability is most commonly defined as meeting the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.&nbsp;&nbsp; The field of Sustainability Studies explores and critiques ways to do this in the context of maintaining ecological and environmental health while generating economic welfare and ensuring social justice.&nbsp; The minor in Sustainability Studies integrates across disciplines with its goal of instilling understanding, appreciation, and thoughtful problem solving skills in students about the relationships and interactions of human society and the environment.&nbsp; The Sustainability Studies Minor will highlight issues related to sustainability and explore how those issues impact and are addressed at local, national, and global levels.&nbsp; This minor will provide the interdisciplinary background that is needed to understand many of the complex challenges facing our world, and will equip students with the skills needed to develop solutions to these problems. An additional goal is to build a community of faculty and students with a diversity of perspectives and areas of expertise committed to seeking creative solutions for sustainability.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              Minor requirements
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Students take 6 courses from throughout the curriculum: 2 theory/representation courses, 2 sustainability in practice courses, 1 course focused at the community level, and 1 course focused on sustainable solutions related to specific problems or issues.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Sustainability: Theory and Representation
            </strong>
            (2 courses) &ndash; These courses primarily focus on theory including basic science and social science, the representation of nature and sustainability, and environmental ethics and history.&nbsp; They provide the framework and context for further work in Sustainability Studies.&nbsp; Students take 2 courses listed below, one must be in the Sciences (BIO, CHM, ESC), and one must be from the Humanities or Social Sciences (ATH, ECN, ENG, HST, PHL, SOC).
           </p>
           <p>
            ATH 230 &ndash; Native American Ecology
            <br />
            ATH 310 &ndash; Human Ecology
            <br />
            ESC 111 &ndash; Topics in Environmental Science
            <br />
            ESC 113 &ndash; Environmental Science I
            <br />
            BIO&nbsp; 111 &ndash; Concepts of Biology: Crisis Earth
            <br />
            BIO 260 &ndash; Field Botany and Plant Ecology
            <br />
            BIO 268 &ndash; Freshwater Ecology
            <br />
            BIO 270 &ndash; Ecology
            <br />
            CHM 101 &ndash; Chemistry of the Environment
            <br />
            ECN 245, 246 &ndash; Environmental Economics
            <br />
            ECN 249, 250 &ndash; Economics of natural resources and sustainability
            <br />
            ENG 260 &ndash; Literature and the Environment
            <br />
            ENG 338, 339 &ndash; City, frontier, &amp; empire in American Literature
            <br />
            ENG 357 &ndash; Native American Literature
            <br />
            HST 341, 342 &ndash; Environmental History of the US
            <br />
            PHL 246 &ndash; Environmental Philosophy
            <br />
            SOC 340 &ndash; Sociology of Development
            <br />
            SOC 370 &ndash; Sociology of Food
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Sustainability in Practice
            </strong>
            (2 courses) &ndash; These courses have an applied focus, and offer a substantial research or service-learning component related to sustainability and the environment.&nbsp; Although these courses may also cover theoretical aspects, they are differentiated from the above group by providing both hands-on experiences and more focus on the application of theory and basic science. Students take two courses listed below from two different departments or programs.
           </p>
           <p>
            BIO 262 - Cultural and Economic Botany
            <br />
            BIO 275-279 &ndash; Field Investigations in Biology
            <br />
            BIO 465 &ndash; Conservation Biology
            <br />
            COM 336, 337 &ndash; Environmental Communication
            <br />
            PSC 216, 217 &ndash; Environmental Politics and Policy Making
            <br />
            PHY 180 &ndash; Energy and the Environment
            <br />
            SOC 320 &ndash; Environmental Sociology
            <br />
            SUS 960 &ndash; Internship
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Sustainable Communities
            </strong>
            (1 course) &ndash; These courses examine sustainability at the community level. They focus on analysis and problem solving by examining a variety of issues confronted by a particular community, and offer opportunities to work with members of that community to understand and solve problems at the human-environment interface.
           </p>
           <p>
            SUS 350 &ndash; Environmental and Cultural Conservation in Costa Rica
            <br />
            SUS 355 &ndash; Climate Change and Sustainable Development in Bangladesh
            <br />
            SUS 365 &ndash; Local Sustainability
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Sustainable Solutions
            </strong>
            (1 course)
            <strong>
             &ndash;
            </strong>
            Either through a seminar course or independent research, students focus on a particular issue such as energy, food, or sustainable design and work towards the development of practical solutions.&nbsp; The focus here is on a specific problem and solutions that might be applied to it on local, national, and global scales. Students are offer opportunities for synthesis and application and actively involve students in the scientific methods of hypothesis development and testing, data collection, analysis and interpretation, and dissemination of results.
           </p>
           <p>
            SUS 405 &ndash; Sustainable Solutions
            <br />
            SUS 970 &ndash; Research
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              COURSES
             </span>
             &nbsp;
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             350. (formerly EST 350) &ndash; Environmental and Cultural Conservation in Costa Rica
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Students explore solutions to the delicate problem of environmental and cultural conservation in developing countries with a focus on Costa Rica. During the spring semester, students develop projects and prepare for the two-week study/research/travel experience to Costa Rica at the end of May. This preparation includes study of the area&rsquo;s ecological diversity; political, cultural and social issues; research skills; and relevant Spanish vocabulary and conversation skills. In Costa Rica, students explore a variety of habitats, live in and interact with members of a small town, and conduct both community service and independent research projects. Research projects focus on the ecology, sociology, culture, sustainability, and public health of the region. One of the objectives of the program is to remove the blinders of specific discipline-based learning and of our own culture to show that if we are to develop sustainable solutions.&nbsp;
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             355. Climate Change and Sustainable Development in Bangladesh &nbsp;
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            This team-taught course examines the impact of climate change on economic, social and political development by focusing on the nation of Bangladesh.&nbsp;&nbsp; In addition to introducing students to the science and politics of climate change, the course also examines the specific environmental, economic and social challenges confronting Bangladesh&rsquo;s political development since its independence; investigates the environmental and social consequences of state-sponsored development strategies, especially recent market-based &ldquo;neo-liberal&rdquo; policies; and evaluates new &ldquo;sustainable development&rdquo; strategies that are emerging as a response to the challenges posed by climate change.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             365. Local Sustainability
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            This course will take an interdisciplinary approach to analyzing sustainability at the local level either in the Muhlenberg community or communities in the Lehigh Valley, and will explore human-environment issues within the context of the relationship among individuals and institutions.&nbsp; Through readings, presentations, and the development of individual research projects, students will assess current sustainability efforts and develop recommendations for future efforts.&nbsp; Research projects may include surveying community members about perceptions and attitudes towards sustainability and environmental issues, assessing current sustainability efforts, and interviewing local experts, members of organizations and politicians. Priorities for research will be determined in collaboration with the community, and results and recommendations will be shared.&nbsp;
            <span class="italic">
             Prerequisite: Any single course in the Sustainability Studies Minor
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             405. Sustainable Solutions
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            An interdisciplinary approach to developing and assessing sustainable solutions to meet the needs of human society and the environment on local, regional, and global scales.&nbsp; Each offering of this course will focus on a particular issue such as food, energy, green design, or waste production.&nbsp; Students will read the trade and primary literature as it relates to each specific theme, and then through project-based learning will design and test potential solutions that might help achieve sustainability.&nbsp; Students will study the issues and focus on design, data collection, and dissemination related to solving the problem being analyzed.&nbsp;
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="italic">
             Prerequisite: Any two courses in the Sustainability Studies Minor
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             960. (Formerly EST 960) Sustainability Studies Internship
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Internships arranged with local, national, and international public or private organizations in the areas of development, environment, and sustainability in practice.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             970 (formerly EST 970) - Sustainability Studies Research
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Faculty mentored student research focusing on sustainable problem solving emphasizing design, data collection, and dissemination.
           </p>
