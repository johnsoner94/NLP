          <h1>
           Additional Information for Tenure-Track Position in Biology
          </h1>
          <p>
           The Biology Department of Muhlenberg College is conducting a search for a tenure-track Assistant Professor position to begin in August 2017.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Original Job Advertisement:
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            ASSISTANT PROFESSOR
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Biologist
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Muhlenberg College Biology Department invites applications for a tenure-track Assistant Professor position beginning August 2017. We are seeking candidates who are strongly committed to undergraduate teaching and research in a liberal arts college environment.&nbsp; Teaching responsibilities include an upper-level microbiology course and an upper-level course supporting the biology major or a related interdisciplinary major (e.g., biochemistry, environmental science, or public health). The successful candidate will also contribute to at least one of the following: our introductory biology sequence, a first-year seminar, a non-majors interdisciplinary cluster course, or a human difference &amp; global engagement course. Muhlenberg College is an equal opportunity employer committed to recruiting and retaining outstanding faculty and staff from racial and ethnic groups that have been traditionally underrepresented in higher education. For additional information about Muhlenberg's commitment to diversity and inclusion, applicants can find the latest updates to the College's Diversity Strategic Plan at this link:
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/president/initiatives/diversityatmuhlenberg/">
            http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/president/initiatives/diversityatmuhlenberg/
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Qualifications include: a Ph.D., a record of excellent teaching, and an active research program that can involve undergraduate students. Post-doctoral research or similar experience expected. We are especially interested in applications from candidates with experience in emerging areas of study such as the microbiome, host-microbe interactions, pathogenesis mechanisms, or microbial evolution and ecology. Muhlenberg is a highly selective liberal arts college located in southeastern PA within commuting distance of the New York City and Philadelphia areas. Applicants should submit electronic copies of a letter of application that includes an explanation of how the candidate will contribute to Muhlenberg's efforts to become a more diverse and inclusive community, curriculum vitae, statement of teaching and research interests, evidence of teaching excellence, and sample publications. These and three confidential letters of reference must be sent to
           <a href="mailto:microbiologist@muhlenberg.edu">
            microbiologist@muhlenberg.edu
           </a>
           . Chair of the search is Dr. Marten Edwards, Biology Department, Muhlenberg College, Allentown, PA 18104.&nbsp; For detailed application information visit
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/biology/microbiologist">
            http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/biology/microbiologist
           </a>
           .&nbsp;
          </p>
          <p>
           Review of applications will begin October 3, 2016 and continue until the position is filled.&nbsp;
          </p>
          <p>
           &nbsp;
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Detailed information about the setting and position:
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           1. We are conducting a broad search, although demonstrated experience in and enthusiasm for teaching an upper-level microbiology course is a critical aspect of the teaching responsibilities. We are fairly flexible about what other courses the candidate might teach. We recommend you familiarize yourself with what courses we already offer, who teaches them, and how you might complement existing offerings.
          </p>
          <p>
           2. The teaching load is 3-3 at Muhlenberg (three course units each semester). Because teaching labs and recitations count, most faculty in the sciences end up teaching two courses each semester.
          </p>
          <p>
           3. The College tends to operate as a single academic community. Most of us have friends and collaborators outside the sciences. Candidates should be prepared to talk about how they would contribute to the larger academic community through writing courses, diversity courses, and interdisciplinary courses or experiences that would be very unusual in a large research setting.
          </p>
          <p>
           4. At Muhlenberg, like most liberal arts colleges, the students are all undergraduates. It is unusual for postdocs to be found in this setting.
          </p>
          <p>
           5. In addition to teaching, faculty are evaluated on research productivity and service. Peer-reviewed publication is the expectation. Some faculty members have extramural grant support and some do not. Most faculty members spend their summers engaged in full-time laboratory or field research, typically with a few undergraduates working with us. Service normally includes working on at least one college committee, which provides an additional opportunity for faculty to have productive relationships with other faculty outside the sciences, as well as serving in a variety of departmental and college advising and assessment roles.
          </p>
          <p>
           6.
           <span>
            Contribution to the College&rsquo;s diversity mission&nbsp;
           </span>
           is a critical component of this and all new faculty positions at Muhlenberg. You can learn more about this by reading our&nbsp;
           <a href="/main/aboutus/president/initiatives/diversityatmuhlenberg/statementondiversity/">
            Statement on Diversity
           </a>
           . Note that
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            all candidates
           </span>
           need to address how they will contribute to Muhlenberg's efforts to become a more diverse and inclusive community in their letter of application. Failing to do so will necessarily compromise consideration of your application.
          </p>
          <p>
           7. Familiarize yourself with the
           <a href="/main/academics/biology/facultystaff/">
            current faculty in the Biology Department
           </a>
           to help you articulate how you will contribute to the existing strengths of the department.
          </p>
          <p>
           8. Make every effort to ensure that your file is complete by October 3, since we expect to be deciding on interviews before the end of October.
          </p>
          <p>
           &nbsp;
          </p>
