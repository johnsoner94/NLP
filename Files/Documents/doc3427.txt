          <h1>
          </h1>
          <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="EditMainContent" -->
          <div class="content-add10">
           <div class="center">
            <img src="/media/contentassets/images/about/cfe/aftermath_sm.jpg" alt="Center for Ethics" style="     " />
            <br />
            <a href="/cultural/ethics/pubs/aftermath2.html">
             click for details
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             AFTERMATH:
             <br />
             THINKING AFTER SEPTEMBER 11TH
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Occasional Papers of the Center for Ethics and Leadership
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Muhlenberg College
            <br />
            Volume 1, Number 1
            <br />
            Spring 2002
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             FIFTEEN MUHLENBERG PROFESSORS TAKE ON THE ATTACKS, THE WAR, AND EVERYTHING IN BETWEEN.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            In the immediate wake of the terrorist attacks on September 11th, 2001, the Center for Ethics at Muhlenberg College invited its faculty to contribute essays on the subject to a unique volume entitled,
            <span class="italic">
             Aftermath: Thinking After September 11th
            </span>
            . These essays, written by faculty from across the curriculum, are at times reflective, critical, analytical, and passionate, but always thoughtful and accessible to a general audience.The inaugural issue of the Center for Ethics&rsquo; Occasional Papers,
            <span class="italic">
             Aftermath
            </span>
            is written in a style that is devoid of cumbersome scholarly notation and alienating academic language. Through this collection of reflections, thoughts, analyses, opinions, and questions, it is hoped that the reader will join the Muhlenberg Community in sorting out what last September, and its aftermath, means.
           </p>
           <p>
            Featured on the cover of this beautifully softbound volume is a Joseph Elliott black-and-white photograph of the stunningly beautiful September 11th memorial that was erected in the College&rsquo;s Center for the Arts in the Fall of 2001.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="/cultural/ethics/pubs/aftermath2.html">
             Learn more about
             <span class="italic">
              Aftermath
             </span>
             !
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            To
            <strong>
             order your copy
            </strong>
            , please send a check for the appropriate amount per issue (see below) to:
           </p>
           <p>
            Dr. Judy Ridner
            <br />
            Director Center for Ethics
            <br />
            Muhlenberg College
            <br />
            2400 Chew Street
            <br />
            Allentown, Pennsylvania 18104
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Price per volume:
            </strong>
            <br />
            $3 for Muhlenberg employees, alums, and students
            <br />
            $5 for other friends of the College
           </p>
