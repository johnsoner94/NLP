  <meta property="og:description" content="In many, many ways, Muhlenberg prepared me excellently for life in the rehearsal hall. The theatre I work for, Hedgerow Theatre (see earlier post for more!) is an SPT, meaning that we have an arrangement with Actor&rsquo;s Equity allowing us to use union actors in some (but not all) of our performances. &nbsp;This means we run by equity rules, much like Muhlenberg. &nbsp;Five&rsquo;s and Ten&rsquo;s are regular. &nbsp;Rehearsal is daily (Mondays off). And yes, 10 of 12 (10 hours of rehearsal in a 12-hour period) exists.

 What is different from rehearsals at Muhlenberg is that you don&rsquo;t know who you&rsquo;re working with or what you&rsquo;re working on before rehearsals start. &nbsp;When you walk into an audition you may not know the people on the other side of the table and the direction in which they&rsquo;re taking the project &ndash; also true of the first day of rehearsal. &nbsp; &nbsp;

 Typically, you get three weeks of rehearsal, one week of tech, previews and opening. &nbsp;At Hedgerow, we have two previews before an opening, whereas other theaters in the area, such as the Arden, have multiple weeks of previews. &nbsp;Rehearsals are also far less regularly scheduled than the mainstage rehearsals at Berg. &nbsp;It&rsquo;s much more like trying to schedule rehearsals in Directing Class and its midterms. &nbsp;Virtually everyone involved with a show has another job, so you may have an erratic rehearsal schedule. 

 All this is to say &ndash; it gets a little less organized once you venture outside of the Bubble. &nbsp;To combat this be AS PREPARED AS POSSIBLE. &nbsp;The best approach to any rehearsal is to be ready to go as much as possible whenever possible. &nbsp;That means in your shoes and rehearsal jacket before the start of rehearsal with your lines off book and your body warmed up. 

 Usually, everyone is on the same page and ready to work, which makes rehearsals as rewarding as possible. &nbsp;In the most recent show I rehearsed, Dracula, our director, the inimitable Dan Hodge, was acting in a production that opened the same week as we did, and so we had three hour morning rehearsals the week we opened. &nbsp;That&rsquo;s a small amount of time to get a lot of work done. We had to be on our game and ready as soon as it was time so that we were able to fit a run, notes and fixes in before the end of rehearsal.

 Working with such an amazing, professional and dedicated group of artists, as was the case with Dracula, was a real privilege. &nbsp;Amongst our numbers are &rsquo;06 graduate Meghan Winch and &rsquo;14 graduate Josh Portera (also a company member at Hedgerow). &nbsp;We were able to create a fantastic production with a limited rehearsal period due to the tenacity and readiness of everyone involved.

 For more info on Hedgerow Theatre or to get tickets for Dracula visit www.HedgerowTheatre.org (Student/Educator tickets $15). &nbsp;For more info on what I&rsquo;m up to visit www.allisonbloechl.weebly.com." />

        <p>
         In many, many ways, Muhlenberg prepared me excellently for life in the rehearsal hall. The theatre I work for, Hedgerow Theatre (see earlier post for more!) is an SPT, meaning that we have an arrangement with Actor&rsquo;s Equity allowing us to use union actors in some (but not all) of our performances. &nbsp;This means we run by equity rules, much like Muhlenberg. &nbsp;Five&rsquo;s and Ten&rsquo;s are regular. &nbsp;Rehearsal is daily (Mondays off). And yes, 10 of 12 (10 hours of rehearsal in a 12-hour period) exists.
        </p>
        <p>
         What is different from rehearsals at Muhlenberg is that you don&rsquo;t know who you&rsquo;re working with or what you&rsquo;re working on before rehearsals start. &nbsp;When you walk into an audition you may not know the people on the other side of the table and the direction in which they&rsquo;re taking the project &ndash; also true of the first day of rehearsal. &nbsp; &nbsp;
        </p>
        <p>
         Typically, you get three weeks of rehearsal, one week of tech, previews and opening. &nbsp;At Hedgerow, we have two previews before an opening, whereas other theaters in the area, such as the Arden, have multiple weeks of previews. &nbsp;Rehearsals are also far less regularly scheduled than the mainstage rehearsals at Berg. &nbsp;It&rsquo;s much more like trying to schedule rehearsals in Directing Class and its midterms. &nbsp;Virtually everyone involved with a show has another job, so you may have an erratic rehearsal schedule.
        </p>
        <p>
         All this is to say &ndash; it gets a little less organized once you venture outside of the Bubble. &nbsp;To combat this be AS PREPARED AS POSSIBLE. &nbsp;The best approach to any rehearsal is to be ready to go as much as possible whenever possible. &nbsp;That means in your shoes and rehearsal jacket before the start of rehearsal with your lines off book and your body warmed up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a>
         </a>
         Usually, everyone is on the same page and ready to work, which makes rehearsals as rewarding as possible. &nbsp;In the most recent show I rehearsed,
         <i>
          Dracula
         </i>
         , our director, the inimitable Dan Hodge, was acting in a production that opened the same week as we did, and so we had three hour morning rehearsals the week we opened. &nbsp;That&rsquo;s a small amount of time to get a lot of work done. We had to be on our game and ready as soon as it was time so that we were able to fit a run, notes and fixes in before the end of rehearsal.
        </p>
        <p>
         Working with such an amazing, professional and dedicated group of artists, as was the case with
         <i>
          Dracula,
         </i>
         was a real privilege. &nbsp;Amongst our numbers are &rsquo;06 graduate Meghan Winch and &rsquo;14 graduate Josh Portera (also a company member at Hedgerow). &nbsp;We were able to create a fantastic production with a limited rehearsal period due to the tenacity and readiness of everyone involved.
        </p>
