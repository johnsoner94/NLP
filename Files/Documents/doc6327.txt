          <img src="../../../../images/main/academics/theatre-dance/panel-cherry.jpg" alt="THE CHERRY ORCHARD" width="340" height="340" hspace="12" style="margin-bottom:24px;" align="right" />
          <p style="font-size:200%; line-height:110%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <strong>
            The Cherry Orchard
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <b>
            by Anton Chekhov
            <br />
            directed by Matthew Moore
            <br />
            featuring faculty spotlight
            <br />
            performance by Holly Cate
           </b>
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <b>
            April 26-29, 2017
            <br />
            Baker Theatre
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           At  the start of the twentieth century, as Russia hurtles toward social and  political upheaval, a widowed aristocrat returns home after years abroad to her  family&rsquo;s estate, which is on the verge&nbsp;of financial ruin. Faced with the  prospect of losing their beautiful cherry orchard and the world it seemed to  represent, she and her brother cast about for a path forward — but they are too  deeply immersed in
           <strong>
            memories
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            lost dreams
           </strong>
           of their youth to take control of  their shifting fortunes.
          </p>
          <p>
           A  bittersweet comedy about
           <strong>
            love, loss, and social change
           </strong>
           , Chekhov&rsquo;s last play  offers a
           <strong>
            rich tapestry
           </strong>
           of characters, interwoven with
           <strong>
            poignant nostalgia
           </strong>
           and  wry commentary on a society on the brink of revolution.
           <!--&lt;p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;"&gt;&lt;a href="photos/WintersTalePhotos.html"&gt;Production Photos&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;-->

that jazz!&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
