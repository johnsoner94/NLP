     <div class="titlerow" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      Is my article Peer-Reviewed?
     </div>
     <div style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      <p>
       Journal articles that are peer-reviewed have been critiqued by other experts before publication.
      </p>
      <p>
       To determine if an article is peer-reviewed or not, you need to check if the
       <strong>
        journal
       </strong>
       in which the article was published is peer-reviewed. You can do this by using the database
       <a href="http://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/">
        Ulrich's web
       </a>
       . For instance, in the following citation:
      </p>
      <p>
       Barthlott, W, Wiersch, S, Čolić, Z, &amp; Koch, K 2009, 'Classification of trichome types within species of the water fern Salvinia, and ontogeny of the egg-beater trichomes',
       <i>
        Botany
       </i>
       , 87, 9, pp. 830-836.
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Botany
       </em>
       is the journal title. Using
       <a href="http://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/">
        Ulrich's web
       </a>
       , type in the name of the journal, in this case&nbsp;
       <em>
        Botany.
       </em>
       (Do NOT enter the title of the article.) If the journal is listed as "refereed" (another word for peer-reviewed), your article is in a peer-reviewed journal. Therefore, most articles in this journal will be peer-reviewed. Exceptions to peer-reviewed articles in a peer-reviewed journal include news items, commentaries, and letters to the editor.
      </p>
      <p>
       In some databases, such as
       <em>
        Academic Search Complete
       </em>
       , you can click on the name of the journal and the database will list information about the journal, including if the journal is peer-reviewed or not. Other databases allow you to limit to see only results that are peer-reviewed. To determine if you can limit to peer-reviewed journals, look in the database's help section.
      </p>
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Ulrich's Web
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://ulrichsweb.serialssolutions.com/
       </a>
       <br />
       Ulrich's Directory is a database that provides bibliographic information, including if a journal is refereed or not (refereed is another word for peer-reviewed) on international periodicals.
      </li>
     </ul>

   <h2>
    Getting the Full-Text of an Article
   </h2>
   <div id="content_box_10390246" class="content_box content_box_1">
    <div style="display:block;">
     <div class="titlerow" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      How do I get the Full-text of my article?
     </div>
     <div style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      <p>
       To get the full-text of an article:
      </p>
      <p>
       - If you are in a library database with "Get-it" buttons, click on "Get-it." This button will take you the full text of your article or tell you that the full text cannot be found. If the full text is not available from Trexler Library,
       <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/services/interlibrary-loan.html">
        interlibrary loan
       </a>
       the book or article.
      </p>
      <p>
       - If you have citation information for the article (title, author, etc.), type the information about your article into
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.on.worldcat.org/discovery">
        Encompass
       </a>
       . Find the article on the results list, and click on the title. If you have access to the article via Trexler Library, a link to full text or view-now will be provided.&nbsp; If not, you can request a copy of the article by clicking the "Request Item" button.
      </p>
      <p>
       - If Trexler Library does not have access to the full text of the article, you can also search
       <a href="http://www.google.com/scholar">
        Google Scholar
       </a>
       by article title to see if the author has made a copy freely available online. Look to the far right of the article's title to see if there is a link to a freely available copy.&nbsp;
      </p>
     </div>

   <h2>
    Finding Data &amp; Statistics
   </h2>
   <div id="content_box_21276486" class="content_box content_box_3">
    <div style="display:block;">
     <div class="titlerow" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      United States Statistics
     </div>
     <div style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      <p>
       This is a list of selected multi-purpose resources.&nbsp; For additional recommendations by topic, see the
       <a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/content.php?pid=471100&amp;sid=3874385">
        Finding Statistics and Data: Topical Sources
       </a>
       guide.&nbsp;
      </p>
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       American Factfinder
       <br />
       <a href="http://factfinder2.census.gov/">
        http://factfinder2.census.gov/
       </a>
       <br />
       A database from the U.S. Census Bureau with population, housing, and business statistics at the country, state, county, city, and zip code levels.
      </li>
      <li>
       FedStats
       <br />
       <a href="http://fedstats.sites.usa.gov/">
        http://fedstats.sites.usa.gov/
       </a>
       <br />
       Links to official statistical information produced by the U.S. federal government.  Browse by topic or agency or search across agency websites.
      </li>
      <li>
       Historical Statistics of the United States
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://hsus.cambridge.org/HSUSWeb/">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://hsus.cambridge.org/HSUSWeb/
       </a>
       <br />
       Statistics from colonial times to modern day covering population, work and welfare, economic structure and performance, economic sectors, and governance and international relations.
      </li>
      <li>
       ICPSR (Inter-University Consortium for Political and Social Research)
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.icpsr.umich.edu/index.html">
        http://www.icpsr.umich.edu/index.html
       </a>
       <br />
       A data archive of research in the social sciences. Specialized collections of data in education, aging, criminal justice, substance abuse, terrorism, and other fields.  Full access available only to Muhlenberg faculty, students, and staff who create accounts using their Muhlenberg email addresses.
      </li>
      <li>
       Lehigh Valley Research Consortium
       <br />
       <a href="http://lehighvalleyresearch.org/">
        http://lehighvalleyresearch.org/
       </a>
       <br />
       Quantitative and qualitative data on the Lehigh Valley on: demographics, public opinion, economic indicators, non-profit sector, governmental sector, and more.
      </li>
      <li>
       Pennsylvania State Data Center
       <br />
       <a href="http://pasdc.hbg.psu.edu/">
        http://pasdc.hbg.psu.edu/
       </a>
       <br />
       Official source of demographic and economic data for Pennsylvania.
      </li>
      <li>
       ProQuest Statistical Insight
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/statistical">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/statistical
       </a>
       <br />
       Statistical information produced by U.S. federal agencies, states, and intergovernmental organizations.
      </li>
      <li>
       Statistical Abstract of the United States
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.census.gov/compendia/statab/">
        http://www.census.gov/compendia/statab/
       </a>
       <br />
       The authoritative and comprehensive summary of statistics on the social, political, and economic organization of the United States.  Current and archived editions (1995- ) are available online, as well.   Old print editions (1886- ) are in the Trexler Library Government Documents section starting at C 3.134:886.
      </li>
     </ul>
    </div>

      International Statistics
     </div>
     <div style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      <p>
       This is a list of selected multi-purpose resources.&nbsp; For additional recommendations by topic, see the

       </a>

       </b>
       <br />

       <br />

        http://www.offstats.auckland.ac.nz/

       <br />
       Links to free official, international statistics resources by country, region, and subject.
      </li>
      <li>
       U.N. Data
       <br />
       <a href="http://data.un.org/">
        http://data.un.org/
       </a>
       <br />
       Search across the United Nation's statistical databases.
      </li>
      <li>
       United Nations Statistical Yearbook
       <br />
       <a href="http://unstats.un.org/unsd/syb/default.htm">
        http://unstats.un.org/unsd/syb/default.htm
       </a>
       <br />
       "Annual compilation of a wide range of international economic, social and environmental statistics on over 200 countries and areas, compiled from sources including UN agencies and other international, national and specialized organizations."
      </li>
      <li>
       World Bank Data
       <br />
       <a href="http://data.worldbank.org/">
        http://data.worldbank.org/
       </a>
       <br />
       Statistics for countries around the world on a wide variety of topics, including education-related topics.  From the World Bank.
      </li>
     </ul>

   <h2>
    Finding Government Documents
   </h2>
   <div id="content_box_21276489" class="content_box content_box_3">
    <div style="display:block;">
     <div class="titlerow" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      U.S. Government Documents
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Legal Information Institute (Cornell University)
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/lii/get_the_law">
        http://www.law.cornell.edu/lii/get_the_law
       </a>
       <br />
       Legal collections include: Federal law, Constitution, U.S. Code, Code of Federal Regulations, Supreme Court, Federal Rules, State law resources, State statutes by topic, and more.
      </li>
      <li>
       LexisNexis Academic
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://www.lexisnexis.com/hottopics/lnacademic">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://www.lexisnexis.com/hottopics/lnacademic
       </a>
       <br />
       U.S. legal information includes: federal and state cases; Supreme Court briefs; Federal Statutes, Codes &amp; Regulations; State Statutes, Codes &amp; Regulations; and more.
      </li>
      <li>
       ProQuest Congressional
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/congressional">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/congressional
       </a>
       <br />
       Information from the U.S. legislature, including full text of bills from 1989 on, public laws from 1988 on, committee reports from 1990 on, House and Senate documents from 1995 on, Congressional Record from 1985 on, Federal Register from 1980 on, and more government information.
      </li>
     </ul>
    </div>
    <div style="clear:both;">
    </div>

   <div id="content_box_21276490" class="content_box content_box_3">
    <div style="display:block;">
     <div class="titlerow" style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      International Government Documents
     </div>
     <div style="margin-bottom:10px;">
      <p>
       Access to government documents from other countries via these resources depends largely on how available each country makes them.&nbsp; Language may prove to be another barrier.
      </p>
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Legal Information Institute, Law by Source: Global (Cornell University)
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/world/">
        http://www.law.cornell.edu/world/
       </a>
       <br />
       The LII collection of world legal materials gathers, country by country, continent by continent, the Internet-accessible sources of the constitutions, statutes, judicial opinions, and related legal material from around the globe (excluding the U.S. material held in the LII's other collections). It also holds resources and document collections of International Law.
      </li>
      <li>
       LexisNexis Academic
       <br />
       <a href="https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://www.lexisnexis.com/hottopics/lnacademic">
        https://muhlenberg.idm.oclc.org/login?url=http://www.lexisnexis.com/hottopics/lnacademic
       </a>
       <br />
       International legal information includes cases and legislation for Canada, EU, Commonwealth, and Other Nations.
      </li>
      <li>
       WashLaw Web: Foreign and International Law (Washburn University School of Law Library)
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.washlaw.edu/forint/index.html">
        http://www.washlaw.edu/forint/index.html
       </a>
       <br />
       Links to foreign and international legal resources and research tools. It's especially strong on finding legal materials from specific countries
      </li>
      <li>
       World Legal Information Institute
       <br />
       <a href="http://www.worldlii.org/catalog/215.html">
        http://www.worldlii.org/catalog/215.html
       </a>
       <br />
       Links to constitutions, laws, treaties, international agreements, and related resources by country.
      </li>
     </ul>
