           <p>
            <strong>
             <em>
              COURSES
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a id="c101">
             </a>
             101.&nbsp; Introduction to Innovation &amp; Entrepreneurship
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Students explore the basic concepts in the continuing processes of creativity, innovation, and entrepreneurship.&nbsp; In the context of the entrepreneurship.&nbsp; In the context of the entrepreneurial enterprise, some fundamental concepts from economics, accounting, budgeting, management, marketing, finance, and operations will be introduced, and students will investigate new opportunities, including client needs, sources of funding, and intellectual property.&nbsp; The course may include readings, speakers, videos, and entrepreneurial enterprise software simulations.&nbsp; Students will develop a feasibility study for a new product or service.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            &nbsp;
            <em>
             Meets general academic requirement SL.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a id="c201">
             </a>
             201.&nbsp; Business Plan Development
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Student teams will identify an entrepreneurial opportunity, select a concept and develop it through the creation of a formal business plan. Students will investigate the competitive environment, conduct marketing analysis and surveys, develop requirements and/or prototypes, and consider the components of successful strategies for marketing, production, finance, publicity, distribution, etc.&nbsp; As a result of the business planning project, students will gain a better understanding of team building and management.&nbsp; The teams will formally present their plans to a panel of potential &ldquo;investors&rdquo;.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            <em>
             Prerequisite:&nbsp; INE 101&nbsp; Introduction to Innovation &amp; Entrepreneurship. Meets general academic requirement SL.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a id="c965">
             </a>
             965.&nbsp; Innovation &amp; Entrepreneurship Practicum
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; As juniors or seniors, students will apply their knowledge and experience in a venture of their own, or work of in the community.&nbsp; The experiential components of the Practicum will be augmented by lectures, presentations, and in-depth interactions between students and Practicum faculty.&nbsp; At completion of the Practicum, students will document and critically reflect on their Practicum experience and present these results to students, faculty, and the community.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            <em>
             Prerequisite:&nbsp; Completion of all other courses for the Innovation &amp; Entrepreneurship minor
