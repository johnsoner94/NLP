          <img src="../../../../images/main/academics/theatre-dance/panel-pirates_000.jpg" alt="THE PIRATES OF PENZANCE" width="340" height="340" hspace="12" style="margin-bottom:24px;" align="right" />
          <p style="font-size:200%; line-height:110%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <strong>
            The Pirates of Penzance
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <b>
            Music by Arthur Sullivan
            <br />
            Libretto by W.S. Gilbert
            <br />
           </b>
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           Directed by Charles Richter
           <br />
           Choreographer: Samuel Antonio Reyes
           <br />
           Musical Director: Ed Bara
           <b>
            <br />
            <br />
            Oct. 28 - Nov. 6, 2016
            <br />
            Empie Theatre
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Swashbuckling
           </strong>
           balderdash is the order of the day in this
           <strong>
            hilarious
           </strong>
           , hopeful, high-seas comic  opera by the great-granddaddies of modern musical theatre!
          </p>
          <p>
           Meet  Frederic, the dulcet-toned orphan pirate apprentice. He&rsquo;s a hopeless romantic  who has spent the past four years living with a band of raucous but
           <strong>
            good-hearted
           </strong>
           and entirely unsuccessful pirates. Frederic is eager to run off  with his beloved, Mabel (whose father is the very model of a modern  major-general), but — naturally — complications arise…
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px;">
           <strong>
            <span class="style1 style2 style3 style4">
             Friday, Oct. 28, 8 p.m.
             <br />
             Saturday, Oct. 29, 2 &amp; 8 p.m
             <br />
             Sunday, Oct. 30, 2 p.m.
             <br />
             Thursday, Nov. 3, 8 p.m.
             <br />
             Friday, Nov. 4, 8 p.m.
             <br />
             Saturday, Nov. 5, 8 p.m.
             <br />
             Sunday, Nov. 6, 2 p.m.
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:130%; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 4px;">
           <b>
            Cast lists by date
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           In order to preserve voices and showcase performers, this production will feature two different casts of performers. If you are attending hoping to see a specific actor, please make sure you get tickets to a performance in which they will appear.
