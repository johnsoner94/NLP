           <h1>
            Theory of Connectivity: Jacob Glass '13
           </h1>
           <p>
            Jacob Glass concluded his Muhlenberg experience as something of a scholastic giant. The
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/polisci/InternationalStudies.html">
             international studies
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/envisci/">
             environmental science
            </a>
            double major was the recipient of a
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/dean-academic/awards/world/recipients.html">
             number of prestigious awards
            </a>
            , including Muhlenberg&rsquo;s Future Alumni Leader Award, the Muhlenberg College Presidential Award, a Muhlenberg College&nbsp;Presidential Merit Scholar,&nbsp;National Collegiate Honors Council Student of the Year, the Morris K. Udall and Stewart L. Udall Scholarship and the Harry S. Truman Scholarship - only the second Muhlenberg student to do so after Kate Bartkus &rsquo;00.
           </p>
           <p>
            Glass&rsquo;s Muhlenberg experience can be summed up as a series of seized opportunities. He credits his activities outside the classroom to a number of faculty mentors, including an early discussion with
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/polisci/faculty/hashim.html">
             Dr. Mohsin Hashim
            </a>
            , associate professor of
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/polisci/">
             political science
            </a>
            and the director of Muhlenberg&rsquo;s Dana Scholars program.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img src="/media/contentassets/images/toc/glass-skateboard.jpg" alt="Jacob Glass on his skateboard for TOC article interior photo" style="     float : right;" />
            &ldquo;When I spoke with Dr. Hashim, before I was even a student at Muhlenberg, it became very obvious that there would be the opportunity to follow your dreams,&rdquo; says Glass. &ldquo;You could pursue whatever project, whatever research, you could dream up.&rdquo;
           </p>
           <p>
            Glass spent his four years at Muhlenberg working with Muhlenberg faculty across diverse disciplines, including Dr. Lora Taub-Pervizpour, his RJ Fellows advisor, in
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/mediacom/">
             media &amp; communication
            </a>
            ; Dr. Rich Niesenbaum in
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/biology/">
             biology
            </a>
            ; Dr. Jack Gambino, Dr. Michele Deegan and most of the faculty in political science; Dr. David Amdur in
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/abe/economics/">
             economics
            </a>
            ; Dr. Herrick in international studies; and former dean of academic affairs Carol Shiner Wilson. He continues to speak with several of his former professors on a weekly basis.
           </p>
           <p>
            As a scholar in the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/rjfellows/">
             RJ Fellows Honors program
            </a>
            , he took advantage of funding designated for independent research to film the documentary &ldquo;Grass Routes.&rdquo; The film looks at the advocacy work of the Friends of Scotchman Peaks Wilderness, an organization dedicated to education and outreach in the Idaho/Montana Scotchman Peaks area, and shows the somewhat unusual coalition of environmental activists and mining and timber companies working together to preserve 88,000 acres of roadless terrain.
           </p>
           <p>
            Glass&rsquo;s film shows an uncommon partnership between interests that have traditionally been at odds; he felt that showing groups spanning a great environmental and political divide working together was particularly important in today&rsquo;s culture of partisanship. The film was well-received and shown throughout the country and earned Glass the David Brower Youth Award for Environmental Leadership in 2012.
           </p>
           <p>
            His advocacy work hasn&rsquo;t tapered following his departure from Muhlenberg. Glass recently completed a Truman-Albright Fellowship at the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars, where he served as a writer and researcher in Washington, D.C. He now takes his research and editing talents to the Podesta Group, an&nbsp;international government affairs firm,&nbsp;also located in Washington.
           </p>
           <p>
            He&rsquo;s also working with Zachary Brown, his friend and fellow Truman Scholar recipient, who is currently running for Montana State Representative in House District 63. Glass has served as Brown&rsquo;s communications director for the campaign, writing all of the press releases and working with the campaign plan to drive a fundraising campaign.
           </p>
           <p>
            He has remained a prolific writer, with pieces appearing in the
            <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jacob-glass/syria-chemical-weapons_b_3937047.html" target="_blank">
             Huffington Post
            </a>
            as well as the
            <a href="http://articles.mcall.com/2012-06-28/opinion/mc-russia-syria-ithink-20120628_1_president-assad-syrian-peace-process-syrian-government" target="_blank">
             Morning Call
            </a>
            and Hartford Daily Courant, and his ongoing collaborations with Dr. Hashim led to a recent publication in Bangledesh&rsquo;s
            <a href="http://www.newsecuritybeat.org/2013/10/perfect-storm-climate-challenges-aquaculture-net-food-security-gains-bangladesh/#%2EUnRBAXBr_nQ" target="_blank">
             Financial Express
            </a>
            . Glass also writes as a&nbsp;Junior Fellow at the American Security Project.
           </p>
           <p>
            &ldquo;Muhlenberg is really special; I haven&rsquo;t heard of anyone really having an experience like mine anywhere else,&rdquo; says Glass. &ldquo;If you have creativity, the drive to think of your own projects and make connections between departments and professors, the resources are there. Muhlenberg puts you in a position to have a truly unique experience, that, in my case, has vaulted me towards a career and whatever comes next.&rdquo;
           </p>
