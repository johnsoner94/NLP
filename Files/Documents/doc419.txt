          <div class="et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_text_align_center">
           <h1 id="proposed resolution">
            Faculty Resolution
           </h1>
          </div>
          <!-- .et_pb_text -->
          <div class="et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_text_align_left">
           <p>
            <em>
             The Muhlenberg College faculty approved the following resolution in support of open access in November 2014.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            The faculty of
            <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu">
             Muhlenberg College
            </a>
            is committed to disseminating the results of its research and scholarship as widely as possible. In keeping with that commitment, the faculty adopts the following resolution:
           </p>
           <p>
            Each member of the faculty and staff grants to Muhlenberg College permission to make his or her scholarly journal articles openly accessible in the College’s
            <a href="http://sal.muhlenberg.edu:8080/librarydspace/">
             Institutional Repository
            </a>
            , as permitted by publishers’ policies and/or copyright agreements. To assist the College in cataloguing and distributing the published scholarship of its faculty, each faculty and staff member will, upon publication of the article, provide an electronic copy of the
            <em>
             author’s final manuscript
            </em>
            of the article, along with the appropriate bibliographical data, to the
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
             Scholarly Communication Librarian
            </a>
            . This copy will be provided free of charge and in an appropriate format (such as PDF), as specified by the
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/library-committee.html">
             Library Committee
            </a>
            in consultation with the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/">
             Office of the Provost
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            The resolution will apply to all scholarly journal articles published while the person is a member of the faculty or staff except for: any articles published before the adoption of this resolution; or any articles for which the author requests a waiver. The
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
             Scholarly Communication Librarian
            </a>
            will automatically waive application of the resolution for a particular article upon written request by the author. Waivers will only be granted after the bibliographical data of the publication have been submitted.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Scholarly articles
            </em>
            are understood to be articles published in peer-reviewed journals. The
            <em>
             author’s final manuscript
            </em>
            is understood to be the last version the author sends to the publisher after completion of the peer review process. While this Open Access resolution only covers peer-reviewed journal articles held in the Institutional Repository, members of the faculty and staff are encouraged also to provide bibliographical data and, where possible per copyright owner permission, the full text, of any other publications (such as chapters and essays in edited volumes, reference articles, non-peer-reviewed articles, monographs and visual, audio, or multimedia works), for inclusion in the
            <a href="http://sal.muhlenberg.edu:8080/librarydspace/">
             Institutional Repository
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/library-committee.html">
             Library Committee
            </a>
            , in consultation with the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/provost/">
             Office of the Provost
            </a>
            , will be responsible for interpreting this resolution, establishing guidelines for its implementation, resolving disputes concerning its interpretation and application, and recommending changes to the faculty from time to time. The resolution will be reviewed by the Library Committee after three years and a report presented to the faculty.
           </p>
           <p>
            The day-to-day administration of this resolution will be the responsibility of the
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
             Scholarly Communication Librarian
            </a>
            or other member of the current library staff designated by the
            <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/tina-hertel.html">
             Library Director
            </a>
            . The designated library staff member will process the publication data and texts submitted in accordance with publisher copyright policy and/or agreement, and process any faculty waiver requests.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             The text of this resolution is adapted with permission from the policy at the
             <a href="http://openaccess.voices.wooster.edu">
              College of Wooster
             </a>
             , whose policy was based on those of
             <a href="https://osc.hul.harvard.edu/policies">
              Harvard University
             </a>
             and
             <a href="http://oberlin.edu/library/programs/openaccess/">
              Oberlin College
             </a>
             .
            </em>
           </p>
          </div>

           <div class="et_pb_toggle et_pb_toggle_open">
            <h5 class="et_pb_toggle_title">
             Submit your author&#039;s final manuscript
            </h5>
            <div class="et_pb_toggle_content clearfix">
             <p>
              In late spring/early summer, the
              <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
               Scholarly Communication Librarian
              </a>
              (
              <a href="mailto:kcannon@muhlenberg.edu">
               Kelly Cannon
              </a>
              ) will ask faculty to send to him citations of all of their publications from the current fiscal year (July 1 &#8211; June 30), to be listed in the Source Book and included in the
              <a href="http://sal.muhlenberg.edu:8080/librarydspace/handle/10718/7">
               institutional repository
              </a>
              . In addition, Cannon will also ask faculty to submit (starting with publications dated Jan. 1, 2015 and later) the
              <em>
               author’s final manuscript
              </em>
              of journal articles only. (While other types of publications may also be submitted, they are not part of the College’s Open Access resolution).
             </p>
             <p>
              The
              <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
               Scholarly Communication Librarian
              </a>
              will then review the copyright restrictions of individual publishers using the
              <a href="http://www.sherpa.ac.uk/romeo/">
               Sherpa Romeo database
              </a>
              or publisher websites to see if and when posting of an
              <em>
               author’s final manuscript
              </em>
              (a.k.a. &#8220;post-print) is permitted by the publisher. Only if posting is expressly permitted by a publisher will the manuscript be uploaded to the repository.
             </p>
            </div>

          <div class="et_pb_accordion">
           <div class="et_pb_toggle et_pb_toggle_open">
            <h5 class="et_pb_toggle_title">
             What is the author&#039;s final manuscript?
            </h5>
            <div class="et_pb_toggle_content clearfix">
             <p>
              The
              <em>
               author’s final manuscript
              </em>
              is the post-peer-review, pre-formatted version of a journal article. This version includes changes made in response to peer review, but is
              <em>
               not
              </em>
              a facsimile of the publisher’s formatted and paginated PDF or print issue.
             </p>
             <p>
              Here is an example
              <em>
               author’s final manuscript
              </em>
              from Harvard’s
              <a href="http://dash.harvard.edu">
               repository
              </a>
              :
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               <a href="http://dash.harvard.edu/bitstream/handle/1/13041342/55245972.pdf?sequence=1">
                Example
               </a>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              Additional examples can be browsed
              <a href="http://dash.harvard.edu/handle/1/2/browse?type=dateaccessioned">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              As in these examples, the College’s
              <a href="http://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/about/staff/kelly-cannon.html">
               Scholarly Communication Librarian
              </a>
              will append a cover page which will include the full citation and a link to the formatted version on a publisher’s website.
             </p>
            </div>
