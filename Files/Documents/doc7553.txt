                 BETHLEHEM TOWNSHIP, Pa. -
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
                   Bethlehem Township&#39;s preliminary budget for 2017 contains no property tax increase, but Township Manager Melissa Shafer has advised officials it may be wise to pass a &quot;small tax increase&quot; over the next two years to pay for future projects.
                  </span>

                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">

                  </span>
                 </font>
                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
                   Shafer, in a budget summary listed under long range forecasting, said: &quot;While the township does not require a tax increase for 2017, it may be advisable for the commissioners to consider a small tax increase for this year and next year.&quot;
                  </span>
                 </font>
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">

                  </span>
                 </font>
                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
                   Shafer said a .25 mill tax increase would raise about $210,000 of revenue for the township and cost each property owner about $23 on average annually.
                  </span>
                 </font>
                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
                   The township decided to focus on long range forecasting after last year&#39;s tax increase with the stated goal of getting a better handle on operating and capital expenses over the next five years.
                  </span>
                 </font>
                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
                   Besides presenting the board with the tentative 2017 budget, Shafer&#39;s staff prepared a 2017-2021 long range budget.
                  </span>
                 </font>
                 <br />
                </div>
                <div class="PlainText">
                 <font size="2">
                  <span style="font-size:10pt;">
