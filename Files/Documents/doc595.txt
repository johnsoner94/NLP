          <h1>
           How did this student earn her way to the area's leading news station? By pairing her experience with a Muhlenberg degree.
          </h1>
          <p>
           Kathy Cruz &lsquo;15, was a Media and Communications student from Pontificia Universidad Catolica Madre y Maestra (PUCMM) a university in Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic.
          </p>
          <p>
           Selected as a candidate for a scholarship program by actor Vin Diesel and his foundation,
           <em>
            One Race Films
           </em>
           , Kathy was offered a hands-on opportunity at New York University in film and TV production, which brought her to New York City.
          </p>
          <p>
           After the program ended and deciding she wanted more, Kathy came to
           <a href="/">
            Muhlenberg College
           </a>
           to further her education in the field of
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/film-studies/about.html">
            Film Studies
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Kathy was hired part time at
           <a href="/">
            Muhlenberg College
           </a>
           as the Multimedia Coordinator in the Media Services Department. This position offered additional hands-on experience using production equipment.
          </p>
          <p>
           As part of the
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/film-studies/courses.html">
            Film Studies program curriculum
           </a>
           , students create a video portfolio to showcase their educational experience in the field. Kathy's short film,
           <em>
            The Nun
           </em>
           , won "Best Drama" at the&nbsp;Muhlenberg Film Association Film Fest, "The Muhlies" and was presented at the International Film Festival, Mujeres en Corto this past March.
          </p>
          <p>
           Set to graduate in May with a bachelor&rsquo;s of arts degree in
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/film-studies/about.html">
            Film Studies
           </a>
           and a minor in
           <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/english/home.html">
            English
           </a>
           , Kathy secured a position at
           <a href="http://www.wfmz.com/">
            WFMZ, Channel 69 in Allentown
           </a>
           . &ldquo;I am an Associate Producer for the Spanish Newscast, WFMZ 69 News Edicion en Espanol. I write, pitch and select the stories for the newscast. I work hands-on with the Executive Producer overseeing the newscast and coordinating every aspect of the show.&rdquo;
          </p>
          <p>
           Part of her responsibilities include line-producing the newscast while the show is &ldquo;live on air&rdquo;. This means she ensures each story is completed on time. &ldquo;I cue the reporters, track every second of the show and work with the Technical Director to make sure that graphics and videos are cued at the right time.&rdquo; Also responsible for investigating stories and writing reports, Kathy produces voiceovers for the newscast.
          </p>
          <p>
           &zwnj;
           <img src="/media/contentassets/images/wescoe/AmicoStudios-2290-460x307.jpg" alt="Kathy on Set WFMZ 69" style="     " />
          </p>
          <p class="style2">
           Charles French, Ph.D. English Instructor/Academic Advisor, who was Kathy's English minor advisor commented on the importance of a liberal arts degree. &ldquo;The liberal arts education helps students begin a lifelong quest to gain and explore an understanding of the world in which they live.&nbsp; This education in the humanities helps students to become well-rounded individuals, capable of seeing life from disparate perspectives.&rdquo;
          </p>
          <p class="style2">
           For more information on the
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/film-studies/about.html">
             Film Studies program
            </a>
            or other programs
           </strong>
           at
           <strong>
            The Wescoe School of Muhlenberg College
           </strong>
           , please email
           <a href="mailto:wescoe@muhlenberg.edu">
            <strong>
             wescoe@muhlenberg.edu
            </strong>
           </a>
           or call
           <strong>
            484-664-3300
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
