          <div class="ndn_embed" id="ndn-video-player-1" data-config-distributor-id="90303">
          </div>
          <p>
           Muhlenberg junior running back and Saucon Valley graduate Nick Savant brings a lot back to Mules football this fall as the team seeks its first Centennial Conference championship since 2010.
          </p>
          <p>
           Savant is Muhlenberg's leading returning rusher with 650 yards and five touchdowns as a sophomore while catching 16 passes for 128 yards and and two TDs.
          </p>
          <p>
           We caught up with Savant at a recent practice in west Allentown and popped a few questions - some serious, some fun - to him.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Who was the most influential coach in your life?
           </strong>
           "No doubt my dad, Lou. He always coached me when I was little, all the way until middle school football. He was always there for me to help me in whatever I needed."&nbsp;
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What's your favorite comfort food?
           </strong>
           "Peanut butter cookies with chocolate in the middle. I love those."
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            How would you describe Muhlenberg head coach Mike Donnelly in one word?
           </strong>
           "Intelligent."
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What is your favorite memory of playing football at Saucon Valley?
           </strong>
           "When we won the Eastern Conference championship when I was a senior (in 2013) against Wilkes-Barre Coughlin 50-49 in overtime. It was great. It was a tough game, back and forth, and it came down to the end of the game." Savant scored the winning touchdown on a pass reception and ran for 264 yards and 4 touchdowns in the game.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What's your favorite TV show to sit down and enjoy?
           </strong>
           "Smallville; we have the DVDs of that and watch it all the time. Or Supernatural."
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What was your prime area to improve on after 2015?
           </strong>
           "Definitely pass protection. I really needed to get bigger and stronger to keep people off (senior QB) Nick Palladino. I really focused on that."&nbsp;
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What's the best part of playing football at Muhlenberg?
           </strong>
           "Getting to spend time with my teammates. This is a great group of guys and everybody's friends."
          </p>
