           <h3>
            External Funding Opportunities
           </h3>
           <p align="justify">
            The College&rsquo;s Development Office and Provost's office assist Muhlenberg faculty in their advancement of professionally significant research. While the Provost&rsquo;s Office provides a number of internally funded, competitive research opportunities, all applicants are strongly encouraged to explore thoroughly the possibilities of external grants and should indicate the progress they have made in finding such support with the Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations Office. Individuals planning proposals for external grants should contact Deb Kipp, Assistant Vice President for Development and Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations, for information and assistance.
           </p>
           <p align="justify">
            The Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations Office is responsible, on behalf of Muhlenberg College, to ensure that proposals are consistent with College policies and sponsor proposal guidelines and that commitments made in proposals can be honored if the proposal is funded. This office will assist in narrative development, budget preparation, indirect cost verification, fringe benefit calculations, cover letters, endorsement letters, securing matching funds, 501&copy; (3) forms, etc.
           </p>
           <p align="justify">
            The Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations Office is the only College office authorized to submit proposals that comply fully with sponsor and university guidelines on behalf of Muhlenberg College. Accordingly the Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations Office is also authorized to elect NOT to submit proposals that do not comply fully with sponsor and university submission guidelines. It is College policy that no external grants, including faculty research and professional development grants, can be submitted without joint approval of the Provost&rsquo;s Office and the Corporate, Foundation, and Government Relations Office.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The American Council of Learned Societies
            </strong>
            <strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.acls.org/" target="_blank">
              http://www.acls.org/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.acls.org/programs/acls/" target="_blank">
               ACLS Fellowships
              </a>
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.xmarks.com/site/www.acls.org/felguide.htm" target="_blank">
              http://www.acls.org/felguide.htm
             </a>
             )
             <strong>
              <br />
             </strong>
             Support for six to twelve consecutive months devoted to full-time research in all disciplines of the humanities and humanities-related social sciences.&nbsp; Amount of support depends on academic rank of applicant.
             <br />
             <br />
             ACLS Fellowships include
             <br />
             <strong>
              ACLS/SSRC/NEH International and Area Studies Fellowships
              <br />
             </strong>
             To supporthumanistic research in area studies, special funding by the National Endowment for the Humanities and the ACLS has been set aside for up to 10 ACLS/SSRC/NEH International and Area Studies Fellowships to be designated among the successful applicants to the central ACLS Fellowship competition. Scholars pursuing research and writing on the societies and cultures of Asia, Africa, the Middle East, Latin America and the Caribbean, Eastern Europe, and the former Soviet Union will be eligible for these special fellowships.
             <strong>
              <br />
             </strong>
             <strong>
              ACLS/New York Public Library Fellowships
              <br />
             </strong>
             A collaborative program with the New York Public Library to provide up to five residential fellowships at the Library&rsquo;s
             <a href="http://www.nypl.org/research/chss/scholars/" target="_blank">
              Dorothy and Lewis B. Cullman Center for Scholars and Writers
             </a>
             . The Center provides opportunities for up to 15 Fellows to explore and use the collections of the NYPL Humanities and Social Sciences Library.&nbsp; Further information at
             <a href="http://www.nypl.org" target="_blank">
              www.nypl.org.
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Charles A. Ryskamp Research Fellowships
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.acls.org/programs/collaborative/" target="_blank">
              http://www.acls.org/rysguide.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             Support for one academic year and possibly a summer for advanced assistant professors and untenured associate professors in the humanities and related social sciences whose scholarly contributions have advanced their fields and who have well designed and carefully developed plans for new research.&nbsp;
             <br />
             <strong>
              Frederick Burkhardt Residential Fellowships for Recently Tenured Scholars
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.acls.org/programs/burkhardt/" target="_blank">
              http://www.acls.org/burkguide.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             Open to recently tenured humanists&mdash;scholars who will have begun their first tenured contracts by the application deadline but began their first tenured contracts no earlier than four years prior to the deadline.&nbsp; Supports long-term, unusually ambitious projects in the humanities and related social sciences, requires an academic year (normally nine months) of residence at any one of the national residential research centers participating in the program.&nbsp; Requires that the fellow&rsquo;s home institutions provide support for an additional period
             <br />
             <strong>
              ACLS Digital Innovation Fellowships
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.acls.org/programs/digital/" target="_blank">
              http://www.acls.org/difguide.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             Support for digitally based research projects in all disciplines of the humanities and humanities-related social sciences, to help advance digital humanistic scholarship by broadening understanding of its nature and exemplifying the robust infrastructure necessary for creating further such works.&nbsp; Support for an academic year dedicated to work on a major scholarly project that takes a digital form.
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The American Philosophical Society (
             <a href="http://www.amphilsoc.org/" target="_blank">
              http://www.amphilsoc.org/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              Sabbatical Fellowships in the Humanities and Social Sciences
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/sabbatical/" target="_blank">
              http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/sabbatical.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             Support for mid-career faculty of universities and 4-year colleges in the United States who have been granted a sabbatical/research leave but for whom financial support from the home institution is available for only part of the year.&nbsp; No restriction on where the fellow resides; in May fellows participate in a two-day, all-expenses-paid symposium at the APS in Philadelphia to discuss their work.
            </p>
            <p>
             Other programs include
             <br />
             <strong>
              Phillips Fund Grant for Native American Research
              <br />
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/phillips/" target="_blank">
              http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/phillips.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             <strong>
              Franklin Research Grants
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/franklin/" target="_blank">
              http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/franklin.htm
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             To help meet the costs of travel to libraries and archives for research purposes; the purchase of microfilm, photocopies, or equivalent research materials; the costs associated with fieldwork; or laboratory research expenses.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Library Resident Research Fellowship
             </strong>
             <br />
             Short-term residential fellowships for conducting research in its collections (
             <a href="http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/resident/" target="_blank">
              http://www.amphilsoc.org/grants/resident.htm
             </a>
             ).
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The Carnegie Corporation (
             <a href="http://www.carnegie.org/" target="_blank">
              http://www.carnegie.org/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              Carnegie Scholars Program
             </strong>
             , which supports individual scholars to conduct research that extends the boundaries of its grant making priorities. For the next few years, the Scholars Program will focus on supporting scholars whose research relates to intellectual and policy developments in Islam and Muslim communities (
             <a href="http://carnegie.org/programs/past-commissions-councils-and-task-forces/carnegie-scholars/" target="_blank">
              http://www.carnegie.org/sub/program/scholars.html
             </a>
             ).
             <br />
             <br />
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The J. Paul Getty Foundation
            </strong>
            <strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.getty.edu/grants/" target="_blank">
              http://www.getty.edu/grants/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              Grants for Residential Scholars
             </strong>
             <br />
             Support for scholars in residence at the Getty Center and the Getty Villa in Los Angeles including Getty Scholars and Visiting Scholars, Villa Getty Scholars and Visiting Scholars, and Library Research Grants.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Grants for Non-residential Scholars
              <br />
             </strong>
             Include Postdoctoral Fellowships, Collaborative Research Grants, and Curatorial Research Fellowships.
             <br />
             (
             <a href="http://www.getty.edu/grants/research/scholars/" target="_blank">
              http://www.getty.edu/grants/research/scholars/index.html
             </a>
             )
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The National Endowment for the Arts (
             <a href="http://www.arts.endow.gov/" target="_blank">
              http://www.arts.endow.gov/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              Literature Fellowships
              <br />
             </strong>
             Support for published creative writers and translators of exceptional talent in the areas of prose and poetry. Creative Writing Fellowships enable recipients to set aside time for writing, research, travel, and general career advancement. Translation Projects enable recipients to translate work from other languages into English.
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The National Endowment for the Humanities
            </strong>
            <strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/
             </a>
             )
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div style="margin-left: 40px;">
            <p>
             <strong>
              Summer Stipends
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/stipends.html" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/stipends.html
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             For full-time work on a humanities project for a period of two months; to support individuals pursuing advanced research that contributes to scholarly knowledge or to the public's understanding of the humanities. Recipients usually produce scholarly articles, monographs on specialized subjects, books on broad topics, archaeological site reports, translations, editions, or other scholarly tools.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Fellowships
             </strong>
             (
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/fellowships.html" target="_blank">
               http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/fellowships.html
              </a>
              )
              <br />
             </span>
             For full-time work on a humanities project for a period of six to twelve months; support individuals pursuing advanced research in the humanities that contributes to scholarly knowledge or to the general public's understanding of the humanities.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Collaborative Research Grants
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/collaborative.html" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/collaborative.html#program
             </a>
             ) For support full-time or part-time activities for periods of one to three years, support original research undertaken by a team of two or more scholars; or research coordinated by an individual scholar that, because of its scope or complexity, requires additional staff and resources beyond the individual's salary.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Grants for Fellowship Programs at Independent Research Institutions
             </strong>
             (FPIRI) (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/fpiri.html" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/fpiri.html
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             Fellowships to support research at institutions devoted to advanced study and research in the humanities.&nbsp; A list of currently supported institutions is available at the web site.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Digital Humanities Start-Up Grants
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/digitalhumanitiesstartup.html" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/digitalhumanitiesstartup.html
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             For the planning or initial stages of digital initiatives in all areas of the humanities, support full-time or part-time activities for periods up to eighteen months, should result in plans, prototypes, or proofs of concept for long-term digital humanities projects prior to implementation.
             <br />
             <strong>
              Summer Seminars and Institutes
             </strong>
             (
             <a href="http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/seminars.html" target="_blank">
              http://www.neh.gov/grants/guidelines/seminars.html
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             These grants support national faculty development programs in the humanities for school teachers, and for college and university teachers. Seminars and institutes may be as short as two weeks or as long as six weeks.
            </p>

            </strong>

            <p>
             <strong>
              Industry Studies Fellowships
              <br />
             </strong>
             To support the development of industry studies, a multidisciplinary field of research on industries that is grounded in direct observation; to recognize and support junior faculty members in a wide range of academic disciplines who show the most outstanding promise of making important contributions to understanding the complex systems of companies, product and labor markets, institutions and their interactions that shape the multifaceted environment of modern industrial enterprises.(
             <a href="http://www.sloan.org/programs/fellow_announ.shtml" target="_blank">
              http://www.sloan.org/programs/fellow_announ.shtml
             </a>
             )
             <br />
             <strong>
              Sloan Research Fellowships
             </strong>
             <br />
             For Ph.D.s (or equivalent) in chemistry, physics, mathematics, computer science, economics, neuroscience, computational and evolutionary molecular biology, or in a related interdisciplinary field, who are members of the regular faculty (i.e., tenure track) of a college or university in the United States or Canada. They may be no more than six years from completion of the most recent Ph.D. or equivalent as of the year of their nomination, unless special circumstances such as military service, a change of field, or child rearing are involved or unless they have held a faculty appointment for less than two years.&nbsp; Candidates are nominated by department heads or other senior researchers. Fellowships are awarded for a two-year period directly to the Fellow's institution and may be used by the Fellow for such purposes as equipment, technical assistance, professional travel, trainee support, or any other activity directly related to the Fellow&rsquo;s research. (
             <a href="http://www.sloan.org/programs/fellowship_brochure.shtml" target="_blank">
              http://www.sloan.org/programs/fellowship_brochure.shtml
             </a>

           </p>

              http://www.si.edu/ofg/infotoapply.htm
             </a>

             <br />
             Support for research in a discipline pursued at the Smithsonian, especially projects that broaden and diversify the research conducted within these disciplines. Fellowships are offered to support research at Smithsonian facilities or field stations. Fellows are expected to spend most of their tenure in residence at the Smithsonian, except when arrangements are made for periods of field work or research travel. Appropriate members of the Smithsonian professional staff must be willing and able to serve in the capacity of principal advisor or host, and space and facilities must be available to accommodate the proposed research.&nbsp;
             <strong>
              Postdoctoral Fellowships
             </strong>
             are offered to scholars who have held a Ph.D. or equivalent for less than seven years.
             <strong>
              Senior Fellowships
             </strong>
             are offered to scholars who have held a Ph.D. or equivalent for seven years or more. The term is 3 to 12 months (note: postdoctoral fellowships in science may be awarded for up to twenty-four months).
