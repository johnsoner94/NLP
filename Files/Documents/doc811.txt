          <h1>
           John I. Williams, Jr. - Biography
          </h1>
          <div class="content-add05">
           <p>
            &zwnj;
            <img src="/media/contentassets/images/headshots/presidentsstaff/JW-Head-Shot-5.jpg" alt="John I. Williams Jr.headshot for biography" style="   margin : 3px;  float : right;" />
            John Williams became the twelfth president of Muhlenberg College on July 1, 2015.&nbsp; He came to that position with extensive, wide ranging and broad experience both in small, liberal arts college direction and oversight, competitive strategy, corporate leadership and entrepreneurship.
           </p>
           <p>
            Williams was born in New York City and grew up in suburban Westbury, Long Island. He is a 1975 graduate, magna cum laude, of Amherst College, where he majored in Economics.&nbsp; Williams then went directly on to graduate school, where he earned both a JD at Harvard Law School and an MBA at Harvard Business School.&nbsp;
           </p>
           <p>
            After passing both the New York and Massachusetts bar exams in the summer of 1979, Williams joined Bain and Company, the strategy consulting firm, at its Boston headquarters; he was the first person of color ever hired by the firm. At Bain, he consulted for several Fortune 100 clients and led a number of case teams for clients across a variety of industries. After five years at Bain, he co-launched Softbridge Microsystems, the first of several successful entrepreneurial ventures that he has helped to grow. Williams eventually joined American Express in New York, where he rose through a number of senior executive positions, including senior vice president of strategic planning, the head of the U.S. Platinum Card business and the head of the U.S. Consumer Travel Network.
           </p>
           <p>
            Hearing the call of the then-nascent Internet, Williams left American Express in 1996 to become CEO of Biztravel.com. He developed this early "dot-com" startup into a Webby Award-winning site serving over 600,000 frequent business travelers by making two acquisitions and raising a substantial amount of venture capital funding before selling the business to then Philadelphia-based Rosenbluth Travel.
           </p>
           <p>
            From 2000 to 2015, Williams led a number of entrepreneurial ventures and engaged in strategy consulting in the non-profit and higher education sectors. He led the consulting unit of Eduventures and served as a partner in the higher education practice at The Bridgespan Group. During this period, Williams worked with a number of colleges, universities, and other educational institutions to help guide their strategy development in the face of a changing environment. He also served from 2011-2015 as an Expert-in-Residence at Harvard's Innovation Lab - mentoring students from all across the university. Finally, Williams and his wife, Diane, served on the Parents' Council of Washington University in St. Louis (where their youngest daughter graduated in May, 2015) for four years.
           </p>
           <p>
            Williams has a distinguished record of service to his alma mater. He was first elected by the alumni to the Amherst College board of trustees in 1984. His fellow trustees elected him to a second six-year term before electing him as a life trustee in 1996. He has remained active ever since, serving continuously on and chairing multiple committees and taking leadership roles on several initiatives that have sustained and transformed the College. Williams has also been a strong supporter of campus diversity, in the broadest sense. He is a former board member of Prep for Prep, a nonprofit organization that identifies New York City's most promising students of color and prepares them to excel at private preparatory schools and then thrive at the best colleges. He is also a former board member of Boston's Museum of African-American History, and a former member of the Alumni Council of his prep school, Kent School in Kent, CT.
           </p>
           <p>
            Most recently, Williams lived in Boston, Mass., with his wife, Diane Pierce-Williams. The two met as students at Harvard Law School, married in 1986 and have three adult children and one grandchild. Diane is a former member of the faculty of the Loyola University New Orleans' College of Law, and a former trustee of Hampshire College (her alma mater) and Goucher College.
           </p>
           <p>
            Williams came to Allentown having traveled the world. He has traveled extensively throughout the U.S. and to over 40 other countries over the years - in North America, Europe, Sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and the Caribbean - either on business, for pleasure or on concert tour as a member of the Mastersingers (a male chorus that, in 2003, won first place in the International Eisteddfod competition in Llangollen, Wales). In addition, Williams is an accomplished pilot, having earned a pilot's license in 1984. He has progressed to the airline transport pilot designation, the FAA's highest and most exacting for both single- and multi-engine aircraft, and has logged over 2,200 flight hours.
           </p>
