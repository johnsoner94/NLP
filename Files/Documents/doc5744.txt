             <h2>
              Career Road Trips Give Students Alumni Insider Perspectives
             </h2>
             <p>
              <span style="font-size: 16px;">
               The Career Road Trip program is sponsored by the Career Center and created to provide select students with a glimpse into the professional world through site visits with alumni volunteers. On Wednesday March 30th, a group of 20 students from all class years headed to Standard &amp; Poor&rsquo;s in New York City for a visit with alumnus Sam Stovall '77.&nbsp;
               <br />
              </span>
              <img src="http://www.muhlenbergconnect.com/s/1570/images/gid2/editor/network/img_5110.JPG" style="font-size: medium; width: 400px; height: 268px; float: right; margin-bottom: 3px; margin-left: 3px; margin-top: 3px;" />
              <span style="font-size: 16px;">
               <br />
               The day began in NYC with Stovall leading the students on a walking tour of Wall Street. &ldquo;His knowledge of the history surrounding Wall St. combined with his engaging personality really warmed students up and provided unique insight to the area,&rdquo; said Ryan Smolko of the Career Center and coordinator of the Career Road Trip program.&nbsp;
               <br />
               <br />
               The students then headed back to S &amp; P for lunch and remarks. Stovall shared lessons and tips from his own career journey starting as a history major at Muhlenberg. Students also heard from Steve Brady &rsquo;98 and Matt Rosenberg &rsquo;08. Smolko reported that the conversation focused on &ldquo;&hellip;how the Muhlenberg network uniquely benefited both Steve and Matt&rsquo;s career trajectories and time at S &amp; P.&rdquo;
               <br />
               <br />
               The day ended with a panel of S &amp; P employees providing insight into the application process and standing out as a candidate. They discussed helpful tips on everything from resumes and cover letters to acing an interview and best practices for LinkedIn. The students were engaged throughout the day and responded very positively. This trip was the first in a series of career exploration visits sponsored by the Career Center.&nbsp;
              </span>
              <span style="font-size: medium;">
               The program will make a return visit to New York in September to see more sites including NBC Universal, Bank of America and The National Arts Club and then attend an alumni and student networking reception.&nbsp;
              </span>
              <span style="font-size: 16px;">
               <br />
               <br />
               "I'm glad the staff and the students got a lot out of the day. Believe it or not, we did too,&rdquo; said Stovall. There are several more opportunities for alumni to be involved this fall through hosting or attending a networking reception. Don't wait. Become a career resource for students today through
               <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/themuhlenbergnetwork">
                The Muhlenberg Network
               </a>
               .
               <br />
               <br />
               <strong>
                <span style="color: rgb(192, 0, 0);">
                 Save the Date!&nbsp;
                </span>
                <br />
                Career Road Trip Student and Alumni Networking Receptions
               </strong>
               <br />
               New York, N.Y. -&nbsp;September 29, 2016
               <br />
               Washington, D.C. -&nbsp;October 25, 2016
               <br />
               Allentown, Pa. -&nbsp;November 10, 2016
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="font-size: 16px;">
               Interested in being a future Road Trip host? Contact The Career Center at careers@muhlenberg.edu.
