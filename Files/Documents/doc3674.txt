          <h1>
          </h1>
          <div class="content-add05">
           <h3>
            Registration Instructions
           </h3>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               What courses can I take?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            Plan right now to meet with your faculty advisor(s).&nbsp; As you speak with them, choose alternative courses to try if your first choices are unavailable.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               How many courses can I take?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            The typical Muhlenberg student enrolls for 4 course units a semester.&nbsp; After you have successfully completed your first term though, you may enroll for up to 5.5 course units.&nbsp; You must enroll for at least 3 course units to be considered a full-time student.&nbsp; Students enrolled for less than 3 course units should contact a staff member in the Office of the Registrar immediately.&nbsp; Please note that most financial aid awards are closely tied to your status as a full-time student.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               Where do I register?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            In your room, in a computer lab, at home - anywhere you can access the web. You may also come to the Registrar's Office (ground floor of Haas) for assistance.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               When can I register?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            Initial registration times are determined by your Graduation Class and by the first letters of your last name.&nbsp; Consult the time schedule chart to learn when the registration window opens for you.&nbsp; You will not be permitted to register if you have an outstanding obligation, financial (tuition balances, parking or library fines, equipment, simplex or fire safety violation bills) or otherwise to the College.&nbsp; All outstanding obligations must be resolved with the appropriate College office (Athletics, Controller, Dean of Students, Library, Residential Services) prior to registration.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               What if I&rsquo;m not seeking a degree?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            Registration for non-degree seeking students begins Tuesday, November 22.&nbsp; Registration for the Day Scholars program will be conducted on Monday and Tuesday, August 15 and 16.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               What if I am requesting an overload?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            Overload requests (over 5.5 units) are made to the Dean of Academic Life.&nbsp; You must complete an Overload Request form (available in the Office of the Registrar).
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             <span class="italic">
              <span class="underline">
               What do I need to fill out?
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            To assist with your advising meeting, complete the Registration Planning Worksheet (available at the Registrar's web site in Forms, in the Office of the Registrar, and from your major department). &nbsp;Please refer to the online course listings and the catalog for specifics about your course enrollments.&nbsp;
            <span class="italic">
             <span class="underline">
              <br />
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="italic">
             <span class="underline">
              <br />
              <strong>
               What if I can&rsquo;t register at my assigned time?
              </strong>
              <br />
             </span>
            </span>
            Your opportunity to register begins when your window opens and will not end until the add/drop period concludes. You may add courses without instructor approval through Friday, September 2 and drop courses through Wednesday, September 7.&nbsp; To add a course after the deadline, you must receive permission from the faculty member teaching the course. &nbsp;If you are unable to register exactly at your time, just do it as soon as you can. &nbsp;If you are not able to register for all of your courses the first time, that is okay. &nbsp;Register for what you can and come back later to add and or drop courses. &nbsp;
           </p>
           <p>
            If you can&rsquo;t be here because you are studying away from campus, you can still register online. &nbsp;You will need to contact your advisor for approval and then add your courses via the web. &nbsp;Contact the Registrar's Office if you have questions and/or problems.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="italic">
             <span class="underline">
              <strong>
               How do I change my schedule once I&rsquo;ve already registered?
              </strong>
              <br />
             </span>
            </span>
            You can change your schedule using online registration any time after you register until the add/drop period ends. &nbsp;You will not need your advisor's approval, but you will need an online course permission from the instructor to add courses that are full, create a time conflict, require a signature, etc.
           </p>
           <br />
           <h4>
            Remember to check for Registration Holds before you attempt to register!
           </h4>

           <p>
            <strong>
             <span class="underline">
              <span class="italic">
               Advisors
               <a id="advisors">
               </a>
              </span>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            The Dean of Academic Life assigns each first year student a Faculty Advisor after June Advising.&nbsp; These advisors are assigned according to the First Year Seminar that is registered.&nbsp; When the student declares a major field of study, the department chair or program director will assign an advisor from within the department/program.&nbsp; The student may also request a particular professor to be his or her advisor.&nbsp; This request may be granted as long as both the requested professor and the department chair/program director agree to such an arrangement.&nbsp; Department Chairs, Program Directors, and the Registrar oversee the reassignment of advisees from faculty who are on sabbatical, other leaves, or have left the College.
           </p>
