          <h1>
          </h1>
          <div class="content-add10">
           <img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/llc/headers/major-minor_plain.jpg" alt="Languages, Literatures, &amp; Cultures" style="     " />
           <br />
           <br />
           <p>
            The Department of Languages Literatures and Cultures offers major &amp; minor programs in the following languages:
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/frenchmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              French
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             ,
            </strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/germanstudiesminor/">
             <strong>
              German&nbsp;Studies
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             ,
            </strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/italianstudiesminor/#d.en.332356">
             <strong>
              Italian
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             ,
            </strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/russianmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Russian
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             &nbsp;
            </strong>
            and
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/spanishmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Spanish
             </strong>
            </a>
            . The major/minor requirements are listed below:
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Major Requirements
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            To declare a major in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/frenchmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              French
             </strong>
            </a>
            and Francophone Studies a student must have a grade point average of at least 2.00 in French 203 (Intermediate French I) or demonstrate an equivalent mastery of the language.
           </p>
           <p>
            Majors complete nine courses beyond the Intermediate I course, including:
           </p>
           <p>
            FRN 204 Intermediate French II
            <br />
            FRN 301 Communication and Cultural Understanding*
            <br />
            FRN 304 Approaches to Textual Analysis*
           </p>
           <p>
            At least four 400&mdash;level courses** (French and Francophone Studies majors will complete a Culminating Undergraduate Experience project in their last 400-level course)
           </p>
           <p>
            After completion of either FRN 301 or FRN 304, French and Francophone Studies majors may count one 300-level FRN course taught in English toward their major by reading in French (where applicable) and writing all assignments for the course in French.
           </p>
           <div>
            <p>
             Majors must maintain a grade point average of at least 2.00 in all courses required for the major.
            </p>
           </div>
           <hr />
           <p>
            Students majoring in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/spanishmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Spanish
             </strong>
            </a>
            must have a grade point average of at least 2.000 in the intermediate language courses SPN 203 Intermediate Spanish I and SPN 204 Intermediate Spanish II, or demonstrate an equivalent mastery of the language.
           </p>
           <p>
            Majors must take a minimum of 9 courses beyond the Intermediate II course, including:
           </p>
           <p>
            301, 302 Conversation &amp; Composition
            <br />
            303, 304 Advanced Conversation &amp; Composition (native speakers may be exempted)
            <br />
            At least 4 courses in literature
           </p>
           <div>
            <p>
             SPN 303, 304 Advanced Spanish Conversation &amp; Composition normally serve as prerequisites to all courses numbered 400 or greater. All majors must maintain a grade point average of at least 2.000 in all courses attempted in the department. The Advanced Conversation &amp; Composition course fulfills the College&rsquo;s writing intensive course requirement for all majors.
            </p>
           </div>
           <hr />
           <p>
            Students majoring in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/russianmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Russian Studies
             </strong>
            </a>
            must successfully complete RUS 204 Intermediate Russian II and any previous language course with a grade point average of at least 2.000 or demonstrate an equivalent command of the language. All majors must maintain a grade point average of at least 2.000 in all courses attempted in the major.
           </p>
           <hr />
           <p>
            <strong>
             Minor Requirements
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            For a minor in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/frenchmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              French
             </strong>
            </a>
            and Francophone Studies a student must have a grade point average of at least 2.00 in French 203 (Intermediate French I) or demonstrate an equivalent mastery of the language. Minors complete six courses beyond the Intermediate I course, including:
           </p>
           <p>
            FRN 204 Intermediate French II
            <br />
            FRN 301 Communication and Cultural Understanding*
            <br />
            FRN 304 Approaches to Textual Analysis*
            <br />
            At least two 400&mdash;level courses**
           </p>
           <p>
            After completion of FRN 301 or FRN 304, French and Francophone Studies minors may count one 300-level FRN course taught in English toward their minor by reading in French (where applicable) and writing all assignments for the course in French.
           </p>
           <p>
            All minors must maintain a grade point average of at least 2.00 in all courses required for the minor.
           </p>
           <p>
            * Prerequisite to all 400-level courses.
           </p>
           <div>
            <p>
             ** 400-level courses carry the College's W designation (writing intensive course).
            </p>
           </div>
           <div>
            <hr />
            <p>
             Students interested in&nbsp;German&nbsp;can minor in&nbsp;
             <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/germanstudiesminor/">
              German Studies
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <hr />
           <p>
            A
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/italianstudiesminor/">
             minor in
             <strong>
              Italian
             </strong>
            </a>
            consists of 6 courses beyond Elementary II, including:
           </p>
           <div>
            <p>
             ITL 203 &amp; 204: Intermediate Italian I &amp; II
             <br />
             4 elective courses in Italian literature, film, art, culture, etc.
            </p>
           </div>
           <hr />
           <p>
            A minor in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/russianmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Russian Studies
             </strong>
            </a>
            requires six courses beyond the College's language requirement, including:
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Literature
            </em>
            (one course is required)
            <br />
            401: 19th Century Russian Literature in Translation
            <br />
            402: 20th Century Russian Literature in Translation
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Culture
            </em>
            <br />
            320 or 321: Russian Culture and Civilization
            <br />
            <br />
            <em>
             History
            </em>
            (one course is required)
            <br />
            HST 263 or 264: Imperial Russia
            <br />
            HST 265 or 266: Soviet Russia
           </p>
           <div>
            <p>
             <em>
              Electives
             </em>
             (two courses are required)
             <br />
             203: Intermediate Russian I
             <br />
             204: Intermediate Russian II
             <br />
             301 or 302: Russian Conversation and Composition
             <br />
             303 or 304: Advanced Russian Conversation &amp; Composition
             <br />
             305 or 306: Readings in Russian Literature
             <br />
             PSC 234 or 235: Government and Politics of Russia and the CIS (or a second Russian history course)
            </p>
           </div>
           <hr />
           <p>
            A minor in
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/spanishmajorminor/">
             <strong>
              Spanish
             </strong>
            </a>
            consists of 6 courses beyond the Intermediate II course, including:
           </p>
           <p>
            301, 302 Conversation &amp; Composition
            <br />
            303, 304 Advanced Conversation &amp; Composition
            <br />
            At least 2 courses in literature
           </p>
           <p>
            Students must maintain at least a 2.000 grade point average in all courses for the minor. The Advanced Conversation &amp; Composition course fulfills the College&rsquo;s writing intensive requirement for all minors.
           </p>
          </div>
