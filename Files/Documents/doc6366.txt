           <h3>
            Abington Memorial Hospital
           </h3>
           <p>
            <strong>
             AN OPPORTUNITY TO EXPERIENCE ABINGTON
            </strong>
            <br />
            Abington Memorial Hospital offers a program which allows students considering a career in medicine to gain first-hand experience in the health care field. A community teaching hospital, Abington was founded in 1914 and serves residents of Montgomery, Bucks and Philadelphia counties. More than 90 resident physicians receive postgraduate medical education at the hospital every year. Abington Memorial Hospital also operates the Dixon School of Nursing, the School of Radiologic Technology and the School of Medical Technology. Our accredited Emergency Trauma Center is the only regional trauma center in Montgomery County designated by the Trauma Systems Foundation.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
            </strong>
            <br />
            In order to gain an understanding of the work and responsibilities of a physician in a modern hospital complex, volunteers are required to work a minimum of four hours a week, including at least one weekend during the summer, in the Emergency Trauma Center. Since most program participants have full-time jobs, hours may be scheduled for evenings and weekends. They also attend a weekly seminar with hospital staff members to become familiar with subjects relating to medical careers. Topics include basic and specialty areas of practice, medical school admission, medical ethics and others. An evening or day in a primary care physician's office is also required.
           </p>
           <p>
            Additional opportunities such as observing in surgery, making rounds with members of the house staff and observing in other hospital departments may be arranged.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             REQUIREMENTS
            </strong>
           </p>
           <br />
           <ul>
            <li>
             Applicants must have completed the sophomore year in college.
            </li>
            <li>
             Applicants must be enrolled in a course of study qualifying them for medical school entrance and achieve adequate grades in these
             <br />
             subjects.
            </li>
            <li>
             Applicants must spend at least six to eight hours a week in the program during the summer (including seminars and volunteer work).
            </li>
            <li>
             Applicants must exhibit a mature attitude toward the responsibilities and privileges of the pre-med volunteer program.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Preference will be given to students living in the immediate hospital area.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             FOR MORE INFORMATION

            If you have questions or would like to receive an application form for the program, contact:
