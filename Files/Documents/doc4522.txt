       <div class="trb_ar_sl_t">
        Meet Muhlenberg College&apos;s new president
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="trb_ar_dateline">
       <time itemprop="datePublished" class="trb_ar_dateline_time" data-dt="December 5, 2014, 9:16 PM" datetime="2014-12-05T21:16:12EST">
       </time>

     <div itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="trb_ar_page" data-role="pagination_page" data-content-page="1">
       <p>
        <a id="OREDU0000129" title="Muhlenberg College" href="/topic/education/colleges-universities/muhlenberg-college-OREDU0000129-topic.html">
         Muhlenberg College
        </a>
        has chosen John I. Williams Jr., a graduate of Harvard Law School with extensive experience as a strategic consultant to higher education, as its new president, school officials announced at a news conference Friday.
       </p>
       <p>
        Williams, who succeeds longtime Muhlenberg leader Peyton R. &quot;Randy&quot; Helm, will be the 12th president of the 166-year-old liberal arts college in west Allentown.
       </p>
       <div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-state="adloader_unloaded" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&amp;topOffset=1000000" data-adloader-position="articleCube" data-adloader-adtype="articlebottomcube" data-adloader-label="data-adloader-label">
       </div>
       <p>
        The 60-year-old Boston resident has a bachelor's degree in economics from Amherst College, a law degree from Harvard Law School and a master's in business administration from Harvard Business School, according to a biography provided by Muhlenberg.
       </p>
       <p>
        He will start work July 1.
       </p>
       <div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below">
       </div>
       <aside class="trb_ar_sponsoredmod" data-adloader-networktype="yieldmo" data-role="delayload delayload_item" data-screen-size="mobile" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&amp;topOffset=1000000" data-load-method="trb.vendor.yieldmo.init" data-load-type="method">

       </aside>
       <p>
        &quot;I can't wait to get started,&quot; Williams told the faculty members, students and staff crowding the Great Room at Seegers Union.
       </p>
       <p>
        Williams is an unorthodox choice for the role in that he is coming from a private consulting background rather than from the faculty or administration of another school.
       </p>
       <div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below">
       </div>

       <p>
        But he said he doesn't lack for experience in the academic realm. Besides his consulting work in education, he has served on the board of trustees of Amherst College for 30 years.
       </p>
       <p>
        &quot;I have not been a faculty member or a provost &#8230; but I have up-close and personal engagement with a liberal arts college,&quot; he said. &quot;I understand the sharing of governance, the focus and engagement of faculty &#8230; I come here already educated in the culture.&quot;
       </p>
       <div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad trb_ar_sntMobAd" data-role="delayload delayload_item" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100" data-load-method="trb.vendor.snt.init" data-adloader-position="sntbanner" data-adloader-adtype="sntbanner" data-load-type="method" data-adloader-label="data-adloader-label" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-v-snt="//content.synapsys.us/embeds/crime/dynamic_mobile/partner.js" data-screen-size="mobile">
       </div>
       <p>
        Williams' non-traditional resume is part of what appealed to the school's presidential search committee, which had spent most of this year winnowing a field of some 500 candidates to a handful of finalists.
       </p>
       <p>
        In an era of technology-driven distance learning, rising tuition and growing skepticism over the value of higher education, traditional residential liberal arts colleges face an uncertain future. So a leader whose main strengths are business acumen and entrepreneurship would seem to be the right fit.
       </p>
       <p>
        Williams made those points during his introductory remarks, saying the two greatest challenges Muhlenberg faces are sustaining its traditional financial model &#8212; supported by tuition and fees, grants and gifts and endowments &#8212; and navigating what he called the &quot;topography&quot; of technology in education.
       </p>
       <p>
        The son of a German-American mother and African-American father, he was adopted as an infant by African-American parents and raised in suburban Westbury, N.Y.
       </p>
       <p>
        He said his own life experience is evidence of the abiding value of a liberal arts education, broadly defined as one that focuses on arts, languages, history and other fields rather than vocational or technical education.
       </p>
       <p>
        Students versed in the classical fields &quot;are more likely to confront future challenges in a nuanced manner [leading] to wiser decisions,&quot; Williams said. &quot;Our nation and our world have never needed graduates of liberal arts colleges [more] than they do today.&quot;
       </p>
       <p>
        Williams also quipped that his mother's German Lutheran background &#8212; the same as the college's namesake, Henry Melchior Muhlenberg &#8212; &quot;may very well have to do with why Muhlenberg feels so much like home.&quot;
       </p>
       <p>
        Eric Berg, a 1979 Muhlenberg graduate who led the presidential search committee, called Williams &quot;the most outstanding candidate in an outstanding group of candidates.&quot;
       </p>
       <p>
        Williams met his wife, Diane Pierce-Williams, at Harvard Law School. She is a former member of the faculty of
        <a id="OREDU0000125" title="Loyola University Chicago" href="/topic/education/colleges-universities/loyola-university-chicago-OREDU0000125-topic.html">
         Loyola University
        </a>
        College of Law in New Orleans and a former trustee of her alma mater, Hampshire College.
       </p>
       <p>
        The couple have been married for 28 years and have a son, two daughters and a granddaughter.
       </p>
       <p>
        Helm, who became president in 2003, announced his retirement in February. He agreed to serve through June 2015 to give school officials ample time to find a successor.
       </p>
       <p>
        During Helm's tenure, the college's endowment nearly tripled, growing to $200 million, and he oversaw a $110 million capital campaign.
       </p>
       <p>
        Muhlenberg also undertook a major expansion of its campus, including the construction of a new life sports center, science building and residence halls; the expansion and renovation of Seegers Union; and the renovation of existing buildings for the performing arts, multicultural life, global education and social sciences.
       </p>
       <p>
        Helms said he was impressed by Williams' &quot;creativity and intellectual agility&quot; and was looking forward to a &quot;smooth, successful and celebratory transition&quot; over the next seven months.
       </p>
