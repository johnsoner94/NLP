               <a href="">
                <b>
                 Hillel International, Open Hillel, and Free Speech at Muhlenberg
                </b>
               </a>
               <br />
               A statement about the recent campus conversations about Hillel, Open Hillel, and free speech on our campus.
               <br />
               <a href="/main/aboutus/president/emeritus/presidentsbio/">
               </a>
               <p>
                <b>
                 Randy Helm
                </b>
                <br />
                Thu, 02 Apr 2015 15:09:00 EDT
               </p>
               <p>
                Recent press coverage regarding &ldquo;Freedom Summer&rdquo; speakers at Muhlenberg College may have created some misleading impressions about how this issue was handled on our campus. This statement attempts to provide a complete and accurate account of these events, and my views on the questions that have been raised.
               </p>
               <p>
                Hillel International, like most organizations, has standards for the type of speakers and programs it will sponsor and those that it will not. The &ldquo;Open Hillel&rdquo; movement objects to those standards and has, on several campuses, attempted to frame the matter as a freedom of speech issue in an effort to force changes in Hillel International&rsquo;s position. Former Muhlenberg Hillel student board president Caroline Dorn &rsquo;15 disagrees with Hillel International&rsquo;s standards and sought to address the issue by proposing that Muhlenberg&rsquo;s Hillel sponsor a presentation by the &ldquo;Freedom Summer&rdquo; speakers. When informed that this program violated Hillel International&rsquo;s guidelines, Ms. Dorn approached another student organization as a potential sponsor. After her proposal was declined by the other student organization, Ms. Dorn sought sponsorship of the &ldquo;Freedom Summer&rdquo; program from the College&rsquo;s Center for Ethics. The faculty leaders of the Center for Ethics could not commit to working on the event, explaining that they were busy with other priorities and programs. Ms. Dorn then resigned her position as president of the Hillel Board. I have been assured that Ms. Dorn was not asked to resign her position and that she continues to be welcome at Hillel.
               </p>
               <p>
                Muhlenberg policy requires that outside speakers be sponsored by a recognized student organization or department of the College. Ms. Dorn subsequently sought support from the College&rsquo;s Sociology and Anthropology Department, which agreed to sponsor the event. Provost Ramsay worked with Ms. Dorn to ensure that moderators, microphones, and security were in place for the event, which was held in the College&rsquo;s Miller Forum on March 26th with approximately 100 students and faculty in attendance.
               </p>
               <p>
                Although rumors circulated that the College opposed hosting this program on campus and discourages faculty discussion of Middle East political issues, this is not true. Muhlenberg has supported and continues to support opportunities for discussion and debate on important issues, including the Middle East and Israeli-Palestinian relations, from a wide variety of perspectives. The College&rsquo;s Center for Ethics, for example, recently sponsored a lecture by Princeton scholar Amaney Jamal on the Palestinian struggle for a two-state solution. In addition to the &ldquo;Freedom Summer&rdquo; panel sponsored by the Sociology and Anthropology Department, the campus has hosted lectures and programs organized by other campus organizations, including Muhlenberg Hillel. There has been any number of programs on this and other controversial issues on campus every semester.
               </p>
               <p>
                The College supports the right of Hillel International to set its own standards and guidelines. Tens of thousands of students and families have joined or supported Hillel with a clear understanding of what it stands for, and what it does not. Just as Muhlenberg does not require the College Republicans to sponsor programs by Democratic politicians, or the College Democrats to sponsor presentations by Republicans, we do not dictate to Hillel who they must sponsor. Nor do we tell our faculty what they can and cannot discuss. Ms. Dorn was entirely within her rights to advocate for a position she strongly endorsed, and to resign her position on principle if she considered that an effective way to communicate her views. Hillel International is entirely within its rights to maintain its guidelines, and Muhlenberg&rsquo;s Hillel is within its rights to maintain its support for Hillel International&rsquo;s guidelines.
               </p>
               <p>
                Muhlenberg College supports the rights of all these parties to express their views on campus, and is committed to the principle of reasoned and civil debate.
               </p>
              </td>
