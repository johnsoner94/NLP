          <p>
           <strong>
            Muhlenberg 85, Moravian 79 -- Rapid Recap
           </strong>
           <br />
           The Muhlenberg College men's basketball team raced off to a big lead Tuesday night and held off comebacks by a fast-paced Moravian squad for an 85-79 opening-night win at Memorial Hall.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Turning point
           </strong>
           : Despite the Mules leading by a double-digit margin just minutes into the contest, the Greyhounds closed within three points at various stages of the game and then finally made it a two-point deficit, 81-79, on a Brandon Maguire basket with one minute left in regulation.
          </p>
          <p>
           Muhlenberg held tough, though, as Brandon LaRose answered with a clutch jumper at the other end to extend the lead and the Mules locked down on defense the rest of the way.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Top performer
           </strong>
           : LaRose paced the Mules with 15 points and eight rebounds, despite missing some time in the second half with a cramp.
          </p>
          <p>
           Central Catholic grad JeanLee Baez added 14 points and five boards while Jon Schreer (13 points) and Owen McLeod (12 points) also reached double digits.
          </p>
          <p>
           Maguire led Moravian with 26 points and 14 rebounds. Bangor grad and Greyhounds freshman Mike Martino saw his first college action, playing three minutes with a steal.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            What it means
           </strong>
           : The Mules avenged a loss to their rivals that ended their season a year ago and got off to a solid 2016-17 campaign with an upperclassman-heavy roster.
          </p>
          <p>
           Moravian displayed a fast-break offense and full-court pressure defense that will give opponents fits this season.
          </p>
