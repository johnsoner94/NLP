        <h2>
         Thanks to all
        </h2>
        <p>
         Hi guys, it&rsquo;s Evan Plaza back at it again with another blog post! &nbsp;So we have just closed our Mainstage Production of The Pirates of Penzance. &nbsp;We have put on this show 8 times, from October 28
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         to November 6
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         . &nbsp;I am not gonna lie, it seems weird to now have free time in my schedule again. &nbsp;I felt like after we had closed the show, I was thinking about the next day when we had to go to call time tomorrow and get ready. &nbsp;This show was my first Mainstage production, and I felt like I have learned so much from this experience. &nbsp;I have learned what it is like to rehearse a show in a month and a half&rsquo;s worth of time, and how valuable that time is to the production. &nbsp;The rehearsal process is so special to any production because it is where the actors get to play and the show begins to mold itself together like a refined sculpture. &nbsp;Having this time determines many things about a show: the tone, the flow, and can even define the overall message that the show is trying to portray. &nbsp;During this process I also learned an essential skill that every actor, better yet, every person, should learn: how to be professional. &nbsp;This show required an amount of time to put together, the time needed to explore and create art determines the success of the show. &nbsp;Having said that, it is important to be punctual, and come prepared to create the art. &nbsp;It is also important to be patient and to come with different ideas to the show; some choices might not fit with the director&rsquo;s vision, or just don&rsquo;t correlate with the characters in the show. &nbsp;I am so grateful I got the opportunity to work on this production alongside a loving and supporting cast. &nbsp;That&rsquo;s all for now, I&rsquo;ll see you next time!
        </p>
