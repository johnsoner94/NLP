             </a>
             National Resources
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Americans with Disabilities Act
              <br />
             </strong>
             ADA Information Line
             <br />
             1-800-514-0301 (voice)
             <br />
             1-800-514-0383 (TDD)

              <a href="http://www.ADA.gov">
               www.ADA.gov
              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             The Americans with Disabilities Act, enacted July 26, 1990 prohibits discrimination and ensures equal opportunity for persons with disabilities in employment, State and local government services, public accommodations, commercial facilities, and transportation.
            </p>
            <p>

               http://www.ahead.org
              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             AHEAD is the premiere professional association committed to full participation of persons with disabilities in postsecondary education. AHEAD promotes leadership and exemplary practices; provides professional development, disseminates information; and orchestrates resources through partnership and collaboration
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              The Job Accommodations Network (JAN)
             </strong>
             <strong>
              <br />
             </strong>

             <br />

              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             JAN represents the most comprehensive resource for job accommodations available.&nbsp; JAN provides information on job accommodations and information on the Americans with Disability Act.&nbsp; JAN&rsquo;s work helps employers, people with disabilities, rehabilitation professionals, and people affected by disability.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              American Association of People with Disabilities (AAPD)
             </strong>
             <strong>
              <br />
             </strong>

             <strong>

            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             AAPD is the largest, national, nonprofit cross-disability member organization in the US, dedicated to ensuring economic self-sufficiency &amp; political empowerment for the 56 million Americans with disabilities. AAPD works in coalition with other disability organizations for the full implementation &amp; enforcement of disability nondiscrimination laws, particularly the ADA of 1990 and the Rehabilitation Act of 1973.
            </p>

             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.naric.com/">
               http://www.naric.com
              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             NARIC is an on-line, information Center funded by the National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research to serve members, health professionals, educators, rehab counselors, students, librarians, administrators, researchers, and other professionals.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              The National Dissemination Center for Children with Disabilities (NICHCY)
             </strong>
             <strong>
              <br />
             </strong>
             P.O. Box 1492
             <br />
             Washington,&nbsp;DC&nbsp;20013-1492
             <br />
             (800) 695-0285 &middot; v/TTY
             <br />
             (202) 884-8441&nbsp;&middot;&nbsp;fax
             <br />
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.nichcy.org/">
               http://www.nichcy.org
              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             NICHCY, a national information &amp; referral center, provides information on disabilities/disability-related issues for families, educators, and professionals.&nbsp; NICHCY provides information and makes referrals in areas related to: specific disabilities, early intervention, special disability organizations, professional associations, educations rights, transitions to adult life, etc.&nbsp;
            </p>

             <a href="http://www.parentcenterhub.org">
              www.parentcenterhub.org
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              The National Clearinghouse on Postsecondary Education for Individuals with Disabilities
             </strong>
             <strong>
              (HEATH)
             </strong>
             <br />
             The George Washington University
             <br />
             HEATH Resource Center- 2134 G Street, NW
             <br />
             Washington, DC 20052-0001
             <br />
             askheath@guu.edu
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             HEATH is a center for information exchange about educational support services, policies, procedures, adaptations, and opportunities at American campuses, vocational-technical schools, and other postsecondary training entities for individuals with disabilities.&nbsp; HEATH publishes resource papers, fact sheets, directories, and fosters a network of professionals in the arena of disability issues.
            </p>

             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.rarediseases.org/">
               http://www.rarediseases.org
              </a>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             NORD is a unique federation (NOT a government agency) of voluntary health organizations dedicated to helping people with rare "orphan" diseases and assisting the organizations that serve them.&nbsp; It is a non-profit, voluntary health agency that exists to serve rare-disease patients and their families. Our primary sources of funding are contributions, membership fees, and revenues from the sale of our products, such as our books and Rare Disease Database reports. Most of the money donated to NORD goes directly to programs and services.

            </p>

NCLD accomplishes its mission by increasing public awareness and understanding of learning disabilities, conducting educational programs and services that promote research-based knowledge, and providing national leadership in shaping public policy.
            </p>
