           <h1>
            A Message from the Chair of the Presidential Search Committee
           </h1>
           <p>
            April 24, 2014
           </p>
           <p>
            Dear members of the Muhlenberg College community:
           </p>
           <p>
            The search for the next President of Muhlenberg College has begun. Over the past several weeks, seeking input from every constituency, Rich Crist, the Chair of the Board of Trustees, has appointed a search committee whose membership includes trustee, faculty, staff, student and alumni representatives. We are committed to an inclusive process and welcome additional input from everyone; we invite you to offer your thoughts and ideas through a voluntary survey which you can find here:&nbsp;
            <a href="https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/MuhlenbergSurvey" target="_blank">
             https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/MuhlenbergSurvey
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            We are also pleased to announce that Park Square Executive Search will partner with the search committee to help identify candidates and to lead us through the search process. The search committee selected Park Square this past week and looks forward to working with two key members of the firm: Jonathan Fortescue, Ph.D., Managing Partner in the firm&rsquo;s higher education practice, and Richard &ldquo;Dick&rdquo; Celeste, former President of Colorado College and a lifelong public servant. Jonathan and Dick will be joining us this week on campus as we kick off the search and then working with us throughout the summer and fall as we recruit candidates who will enhance our already strong reputation for providing a transformative liberal arts education.&nbsp;
           </p>
           <p>
            On behalf of all members of the search committee, let me say that we are extremely proud to serve all of you and to represent Muhlenberg College in this important search. We look forward to finding an excellent successor to Randy Helm.
           </p>
           <p>
            Sincerely,
            <br />
            Eric Berg '78
           </p>
