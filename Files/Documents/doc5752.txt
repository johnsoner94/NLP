             <h2>
              The Future of Media and Technology
             </h2>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p class="" align="center" style="text-align: left;">
              <strong>
               <em>
                <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                 A Conversation with Hulu and Google Veteran Brendan Haney '06
                 <br />
                </span>
               </em>
              </strong>
              <strong>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                By Jason Steinberg '11 and Kate Burdick '14
                <br />
                Written for the
                <a href="http://www.muhlenbergconnect.com/s/1570/index.aspx?sid=1570&amp;gid=2&amp;pgid=1687">
                 Muhlenberg Business Network
                </a>
               </span>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                Facebook and&nbsp;Google&nbsp;are reshaping the advertising landscape. As someone with over a decade of experience, elaborate for us on the changes occurring?
                <br />
                <br />
                <em>
                 The real change occurring is how we, as consumers, are spending our time with media. People are accessing more content than ever on their own schedules across more devices. This on-demand nature of consumption creates an expectation that each interaction with media will be customized and places value on data that can make advertising dynamic and targeted to each experience.&nbsp;Marketers continue to invest in platforms that can target their key audience or create engaging content that integrate their brands into those experiences.
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                What have you learned at&nbsp;Google&nbsp;in your first five months?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 I learn something new every day. My biggest takeaway is the value of every person at the company. They believe and expect that each person on the team can bring something unique to the business that will create positive outcomes for every user that interacts with our products.&nbsp;
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                The bulk of your career was spent at&nbsp;Hulu. Describe the company in 2007?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 In 2007 we were a lean, goal-oriented team. Everyone was very motivated to create something awesome and we all contributed to the varying parts of the business that could use our attention. Our mantra was to "sweat every pixel" and we weren't going to put a product on the market that didn't make us proud. We also had a lot of fun in process; our culture defined our company in those early days.&nbsp;
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                &nbsp;Was it a risk joining Hulu in its infancy?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 Anytime you sign up to work on something new there is some risk, but I wouldn't qualify myself as a startup pioneer. Hulu was joint venture between two major media companies so it was a very calculated risk. The adventure for me was moving across the country and taking advantage of an exciting opportunity.&nbsp;
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                Would you recommend joining a startup today?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 Joining a company in its infancy can be an incredible opportunity to learn about an industry and gain hands on experience building a business. You need to have a passion for the project that outweighs the risk that many startups take on.
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                Why did you leave&nbsp;Hulu&nbsp;for&nbsp;Google?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 I had learned so much from my time at Hulu and was ready to take my experience to a new role where I could continue to learn additional dimensions to my career. This opportunity presented itself and while it was a hard decision to leave a great team who I had gone through a lot with, I knew I had to leap at the chance.
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                Hulu, Amazon Prime or Netflix, and why?
               </span>
               <p>
                <em>
                 <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                  Obviously I am a huge fan of Hulu. The service helps me keep up with a lot of shows that don't fit in my schedule. Netflix has a great line-up of original content that is worth some quality binging. I am a Prime subscriber but I really use that for the free shipping. I also have become very fond of what YouTube has to offer and have found a number of creators on the service that are producing content that engages a lot of my other interests.&nbsp;
                 </span>
                </em>
               </p>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                You started working for&nbsp;NBC&nbsp;right out of college. What was that like?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 I joined NBC as a page. It was an amazing opportunity to work on all the different elements of the business and really see the exciting things the company was doing at the time. I was working with 50 other recent graduates and there was a lot of fun had while we worked some pretty crazy hours.
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
                How was the transition from a media giant (NBC) to a technology startup (Hulu)?
                <br />
                <em>
                 <br />
                 Pretty seamless. I was already working for a digital startup within NBC, brought that understanding of the media business to Hulu. The best part was sitting so close to the technology team and learning about how they built the service and how much effort went into developing a great user experience.&nbsp;
                 <br />
                 <br />
                </em>
               </span>
              </li>
              <li>
               <span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif; color: rgb(88, 89, 91);">
