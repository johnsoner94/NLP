          <div class="content-add05">
           <h3>
            Applying to Graduate School in Sociology or Anthropology?
           </h3>
           <p>
            Here are some things that you should include as part of your application packet:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              Statement of purpose/interest*
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Curriculum vitae* (a.k.a. the academic resume)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Official transcripts* (1-2 copies)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              GRE scores*
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Letters of recommendation (usually 3)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Writing samples* (0-2)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Application fee ($50-100) &ndash; check with the graduate school office of the institution to which you are applying to see if this fee can be waived for financial hardship.
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Deadlines for submission (usually between Dec. &amp; Feb.)
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <em>
             * indicates more information below
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            The Statement of Purpose:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              This is possibly the single most important part of your application
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Should be approximately 1-2 pages
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Should include the following information:
              <ul>
               <li>
                Why you are applying to the department/institution
               </li>
               <li>
                Your broad research interests (and how they fit with the department&rsquo;s overall perspective)
               </li>
               <li>
                Your background in addressing/developing these interests (e.g., fieldwork conducted, papers written, conference presentations, travel abroad, competitive awards, key courses that have prepared you or made a significant impact)
               </li>
               <li>
                Your theoretical approach to the discipline (or approaches/scholars that have influenced your way of thinking about the discipline)
               </li>
               <li>
                Do you have a specific research project in mind that you would like to pursue for your master&rsquo;s/doctoral degree?
               </li>
               <li>
                Which faculty members are you most interested in working with at that department and how do your interests intersect with theirs?
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Curriculum Vitae:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              This is your resume of professional/scholarly activity
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Generally, you should include some variation on the following subjects:
              <ul>
               <li>
                Education (college level, dates of graduation, GPA,
                <em>
                 cum laude
                </em>
                , etc.)
               </li>
               <li>
                List of research interests
               </li>
               <li>
                Honors/Awards (related to your scholarly work; e.g., Dean&rsquo;s List, Phi Beta Kappa, departmental/university awards, paper awards, etc.)
               </li>
               <li>
                Conference presentations/Publications (Most schools will not expect publications at this point, so don&rsquo;t worry if you don&rsquo;t have any!)
               </li>
               <li>
                Research Experience (Participation in field projects, laboratory projects, data collection, etc.)
               </li>
               <li>
                Language Skills
               </li>
               <li>
                Service/Membership in professional societies
               </li>
               <li>
                List of Referees (people submitting letters of recommendation on your behalf and their contact information
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Official Transcripts:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              You will have to request official transcripts from the Registrar&rsquo;s office (they will send them to the schools/departments to which you are applying)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Plan ahead b/c they cost $$
              <ul>
               <li>
                $5 (5-7 days processing + regular mail)
               </li>
               <li>
                $20 (Immediate processing + regular mail)
               </li>
               <li>
                $35 (Immediate processing + overnight shipping)
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Graduate Record Examination (GRE):
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              Testing is year round at several locations including Prometric Testing Center in Allentown
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Online registration:
              <a href="http://www.gre.org/" target="_blank">
               www.gre.org
              </a>
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Score reporting takes approximately two weeks
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              You will be taking the
              <em>
               General
              </em>
              test (there are no subject tests for Anthropology or Sociology)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Cost = $140
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              3 Sections: Verbal, Analytical, and Quantitative
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              You can take it once a month &ndash; your second score is almost always at least 100 points higher than your first
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              Take a PREP course or download some practice tests:
             </div>
            </li>
            <li style="list-style: none;">
             <div>
              <ul type="circle">
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.gre.org/" target="_blank">
                 www.gre.org
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.princetonreview.com/grad/testprep/" target="_blank">
                 http://www.princetonreview.com/grad/testprep/
                </a>
                )
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Writing Samples:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <div>
              Each application will specify whether you should provide writing sample(s)
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              If you are not asked to provide a writing sample, do NOT provide one.
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div>
              If asked to submit a writing sample(s), remember that it does not necessarily have to be a paper written in your discipline. The school wants to see your
              <em>
               potential
              </em>
              as a writer: your writing style, your analytical skills, and your ability to think/write critically about a topic.
             </div>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p align="right">
            <a href="/media/contentassets/pdf/academics/soc-anth/policies/apply_grad.pdf">
             Printable Version
            </a>
           </p>
