          <h1>
          </h1>
          <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="EditMainContent" -->
          <div class="content-add10">
           <p>
            The major in Film Studies requires
            <strong>
             nine courses
            </strong>
            , with at least
            <strong>
             four at the 300- or 400- level
            </strong>
            . Further, no more than four courses cross-listed with any single department or program can count toward the major.
           </p>
           <div class="content-add05">
            <p>
             The requirements are:
            </p>
            <div class="content-add05">
             <p>
              <strong>
               1. Three core courses:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               COM 240 Introduction to Film Analysis
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 201 Film History I: 1895-1950
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 202, 204 Film History II: 1950-Present
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2. One Course in Production
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               COM 251 Fundamentals of Visual Communication
              </li>
              <li>
               COM 351 Video Production
              </li>
              <li>
               COM 467 Advanced Video Production
              </li>
              <li>
               COM 367 Studio Workshop in Television and Film
              </li>
              <li>
               ENG 364 Screenwriting Workshop
              </li>
              <li>
               THR 355 On-Camera Acting
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3.
              </strong>
              <strong>
               One course in national and regional cinemas:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               ENG 263, 264 Post-War British Theatre and Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 325 French New Wave Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 328 Australian Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 330 New Asian Cinemas
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 332 Film Cultures of North Africa and the Middle East
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 334 Bollywood: Indian Popular Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 336 African American Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               GRM 316 German Cinema
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4. One course in genres, forms, and movements:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               COM 346 Exploratory Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               ENG 238, 239 Plays on Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 225 The Western Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 227 Melodrama
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 229 Travel and Cultural Encounters in Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 348 Cinema&rsquo;s Altered States
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 354 Film Noir
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5. Culminating Undergraduate Experience:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               FLM 450 Film Studies Seminar
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6.
              </strong>
              <strong>
               Two Electives
              </strong>
              :
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               The electives can come from any of the courses in the categories "Production," &ldquo;National and Regional Cinemas&rdquo; and &ldquo;Genres, Forms, and Movements&rdquo; or from the following courses. (Again, 4 courses for the major must be taken at the 300 or 400 level and no more than 4 courses from any single department or major [other than FLM] can be counted toward the film major).
              </li>
              <li>
               <strong>
                Note: Production-oriented students should use all of their electives for production courses.
               </strong>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Additional elective courses:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <ul>
               <li>
                COM 440 or 441 Film Theory &amp; Criticism
               </li>
               <li>
                ENG 207 Dramatic Writing
               </li>
               <li>
                ENG 255 Literature &amp; Film
               </li>
               <li>
                ENG 321 or 322 Shakespeare Reproduced
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 250 Contemporary World Cinema
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 349 Film Reviewing
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 360 Major Filmmakers
               </li>
              </ul>
              <strong class="content-add10">
               <br />
               Requirements for the Film Studies Minor
              </strong>
              <p>
               The minor in Film Studies consists of
               <strong>
                6 courses
               </strong>
               , at least
               <strong>
                2 of which must be at the 300 level or above
               </strong>
               . In addition,no more than 2 courses from any single department or major (other than FLM) can count toward the film minor.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                1. Three core courses:
               </strong>
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                COM 240 Introduction to Film Analysis
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 201 Film History I: 1895-1950
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 202, 204 Film History II: 1950-Present
               </li>
              </ul>
              <blockquote>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 2. One course in national and regional cinemas:
                </strong>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <ul>
               <li>
                ENG 263, 264 Post-War British Theatre and Film
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 325 French New Wave Cinema
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 328 Australian Cinema
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 330 New Asian Cinemas
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 332 Film Cultures of North Africa and the Middle East
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 334 Bollywood: Indian Popular Cinema
               </li>
               <li>
                FLM 336 African American Cinema
               </li>
               <li>
                GRM 316 German Cinema
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               <strong>
                3. One course in genres, forms, and movements:
               </strong>
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <ul>
              <li>
               COM 346 Exploratory Cinema
              </li>
              <li>
               ENG 238, 239 Plays on Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 225 The Western Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 227 Melodrama
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 229 Travel and Cultural Encounters in Film
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 348 Cinema&rsquo;s Altered States
              </li>
              <li>
               FLM 354 Film Noir
              </li>
             </ul>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               <span>
                <strong>
                 4. One Elective (FLM 450 is recommended)
                </strong>
                <br />
                The electives can come from any of the courses in the categories &ldquo;National and Regional Cinemas&rdquo; and &ldquo;Genres, Forms, and Movements&rdquo; or from the additional electives listed above under major requirements.
               </span>
              </p>
             </blockquote>
            </div>
            <p>
