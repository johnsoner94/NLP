          <h1>
          </h1>
          <div class="content-add05">
           <h3 style="text-align: center;">
            <strong>
             Continue the conversation begun with the Sedehi Diversity Project by
             <em>
              Digging Deeper
             </em>
             this semester.
             <br />
             <br />
            </strong>
            These events are open to all!
           </h3>
           <p>
            <br />
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Digging Deeper:
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Comfort in Discomfort
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             Friday September 2, 2-4 pm
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br />
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Seegers 113 (The Great Room)
             <br />
            </strong>
            A Common Hour program open to all, this progressive series expands upon themes from the Sedehi Diversity Project (SDP). Participants should expect to explore the ways in which themes from the&nbsp;SDP resonated with or challenged/surprised them and consider possibilities for action. Sponsored by the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            and the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">
             Office of Community Engagement
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Digging Deeper:
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Power &amp; Privilege
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             Friday September 16, 2-4 pm
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             Seegers
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Event Space
            </strong>
            <br />
            Like the &ldquo;Comfort in Discomfort&rdquo; program above, this
            <em>
             Digging Deeper
            </em>
            conversation is a Common Hour program open to all that expands upon themes from the Sedehi Diversity Project. Participants will contemplate aspects of our identities that complicate our relationships with others and connect to our broader context. You don&rsquo;t have to have attended the early
            <em>
             Digging Deeper
            </em>
            program to come to this! Sponsored by the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            and the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">
             Office of Community Engagement
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Digging Deeper:
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Identifying the Structures
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             Friday October 21, 3:30-5 pm
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             Seegers 113 (The Great Room)
            </strong>
            <br />
            Like the &ldquo;Comfort in Discomfort&rdquo; and &ldquo;Power &amp; Privilege&rdquo; programs above, this Digging Deeper conversation is open to all and expands upon themes from the Sedehi Diversity Project. Participants will consider the ways in which broader&nbsp;structures impact who we are, where we've come from and our lived experiences. You don&rsquo;t have to have attended the early
            <em>
             Digging Deeper
            </em>
            programs to come to this! Sponsored by the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            and the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">
             Office of Community Engagement
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Intergroup Dialogue:
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              A Fall Course for First-Year Students
              <br />
             </span>
            </strong>
            You can still sign-up for this 0.5 credit course for the Fall 2016 semester! Students will engage in facilitated dialogues about social identity with other students whose identities differ from their own. Participants will read, view, and discuss scholarly and artistic material about social difference, give voice to their own experiences, and listen to and learn from the views of others. The course meets on Monday evening from 6:30-9:00 pm, and the first meeting is not until the second week of the semester. To sign up, contact your academic advisor and tell them you want to add
            <strong>
             IGD 280: Special Topics: Intergroup Dialogue
            </strong>
            to your fall course schedule.
           </p>

           <p>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Become a
              <em>
               Doing Groundwork
              </em>
              Facilitator
             </span>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br />
            </strong>
            <em>
             Doing Groundwork
            </em>
            facilitators are a small cohort of students, faculty and staff who work toward a campus community that embraces difference and the unique experiences that everyone inherently possesses. During a two-day, overnight retreat and training weekend, participants will engage in dialogue centered on topics of diversity and inclusion. Upon completion of the training, facilitators will design and facilitate workshops and presentations that can be requested by faculty, staff, and student groups. If you&rsquo;re interested in being a part of our 2016-17 facilitator cohort, please fill out an application
            <a href="http://t4.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/leadershipdevelopment/facilitatorform/">
             here
            </a>
            !
            <br />
            Questions? Contact
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/aboutus/meetourstaff/marksmiley/">
             Mark Smiley
            </a>
            , Assistant Director of Multicultural Life!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Diversity Education &amp; Social Justice Strategy Sessions
             </span>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br />
            </strong>
            The
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            is charged with enriching the campus community&rsquo;s understanding of diversity, inclusion and equity. We achieve this mission through a blend of programmatic offerings, including customized workshops and trainings designed to meet constituent&rsquo;s interests and needs. Our staff offers sessions and development opportunities to student organizations, college departments/units, and community organizations on a wide range of topics. If you would like to invite our staff to provide a presentation, workshop, or strategy session, please fill out the following
            <a href="http://t4.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/socialjusticeandequityeducation/sessionform/">
             online form
            </a>
            <strong>
             .
             <br />
            </strong>
            Questions? Contact
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/aboutus/meetourstaff/marksmiley/">
             Mark Smiley
            </a>
            , Assistant Director of Multicultural Life!
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              Grassroots Organizing Workshops
              <em>
               (GROW)
              </em>
              Program
             </span>
            </strong>
            <br />
            Nonviolent direct action and community organizing are often mechanisms that help to create change within and without oppressive systems. The
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            is pleased to offer workshops based on the fundamentals of community organizing through the
            <em>
             GROW
            </em>
            Program. The Goal of
            <em>
             GROW
            </em>
            is to create a culture of organizing as a way to build grassroots power within the College community and create change.&nbsp; It achieves this through student and employee-led interactive workshops and strategy sessions with an emphasis on moving theory into practice. Workshop topics vary from specific skill building such as reaching your base and increasing turnout, to practicing tactics and strategies (accountability session simulation). If you would like to invite a facilitator to lead a GROW for your organization, class, or department, please fill out this
            <a href="http://t4.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/socialjusticeandequityeducation/growform/">
             online request
            </a>
            .
            <br />
            Questions? Contact
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/aboutus/meetourstaff/marksmiley/">
             Mark Smiley
            </a>
            , Assistant Director of Multicultural Life!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <em>
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Alliances For Justice:
              </span>
             </em>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <em>
               Active Leadership Retreat
              </em>
             </span>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br />
            </strong>
            <strong>
             Thursday January 12 &ndash; Saturday January 14, 2017
            </strong>
            <br />
            <strong>
             This retreat will be held off campus. Transportation, meals and lodging are provided.
             <br />
            </strong>
            Many people do not feel prepared to discuss personal identity, biases, stereotypes and other controversial topics that come up in conversations across, among and about communities of difference. So they avoid the conversations. The
            <em>
             Alliances for Justice: Active Leadership (AJAL) Retreat
            </em>
            is a collaborative campus program to help students feel more at ease negotiating these complex conversations and interpersonal situations. To this end,
            <em>
             AJAL
            </em>
            is designed to work with students to create a space for participants to explore their own identities, points of intersection and possibilities for shared action to contribute to positive community change on campus and beyond.
            <br />
            Sponsored by the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/multicultural/">
             Office of Multicultural Life
            </a>
            , the
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">
             Office of Community Engagement
            </a>
            and Intergroup Dialogue.
            <br />
            To learn how to be involved, contact the campus facilitators at
            <a href="mailto:AJAL@muhlenberg.edu">
             AJAL@muhlenberg.edu
            </a>
            .
           </p>
