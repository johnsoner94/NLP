              <h1 style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="color: #ffffff;">
                Through Turbulence to Transformation:
               </span>
              </h1>
              <h2 style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="color: #ffffff;">
                Muhlenberg College in the 1940s
               </span>
              </h2>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               <span style="color: #ffffff;">
                For many years, stories about college campus life during the WW II era were somewhat marginalized in history books. Telling the tales of the brutality of war, the heroism and sacrifices of men and women at home as well as abroad, and the war’s impact on global and national policies and economics took precedence. Even in the small collection of histories about military programs on college campuses during this period (e.g., Herge, 1996; Schneider, 1987; Cordozier, 1993), the focus was the military presence on campuses. While these histories are important to our understanding about and knowledge of WWII era, they also convey and embody the United States’ remarkable dominance to profoundly influence military, economic and political histories. Moreover, these top-down historical narratives, large or small as they may be, are fragmentary records of the past. They leave long-silenced the stories that lie much closer to the lived experiences on our campus, in the hallways, on the basketball court, in the chapel, and in the wartime correspondence with Muhlenberg men on active duty. They leave behind, as Americanist writer and professor Jill Lepore writes, the “thick particularities of an individual’s life” (2012). To that end, students work with Susan Falciani, Special Collections and Archives Librarian, to understand and work with primary sources and work with Dr. Ranieri to develop ethical sensibilities and technological skills to do justice to their subjects as they create short digital documentaries about our Muhlenberg community.
               </span>
              </p>
