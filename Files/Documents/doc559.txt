          <h1>
          </h1>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/italianstudiesminor/">
             Minor
            </a>
            &nbsp;|&nbsp;
           </strong>
           <strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/faculty/italianfaculty/">

            &nbsp;|
           </strong>
           &nbsp;

           &nbsp;|
           <a href="/main/academics/llc/onlineresources/italianresources/">
            Resources
           </a>
           &nbsp;
           <strong>
            |&nbsp;
            <a href="/media/contentassets/pdf/academics/llc/italian/Display Case Images.pdf">
             Display
            </a>
           </strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
           <strong>
            &nbsp;
           </strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
           <strong>
            |
            <a title="Home%20%BB%20Muhlenberg%20College%20%BB%20Academics%20%BB%20Languages%2C%20Literatures%20and%20Cultures%20%BB%20Languages%20%BB%20Italian%20%BB%20Italian%20Prize" href="/main/academics/llc/languages/italian/italianprize/">
             Prize
            </a>
           </strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
           <strong>
            &nbsp;
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Italian Studies
           </strong>
           at Muhlenberg&nbsp;is an interdisciplinary program in a wide-ranging field with deep connections to ancient and modern Italy and Europe, as well as to our own society and ways of understanding and experiencing the world. As the heart of the Roman Empire, the seat of the Catholic Church, the cradle of the Renaissance, the birthplace of Fascism, a theater of both World Wars and a current economic and political pillar of the European Union, Italy &ndash; its history, culture and language &ndash; is of great interest to students&nbsp;of various disciplines. Indeed, the field of Italian studies exemplifies Muhlenberg&rsquo;s mission to expand understanding of globalization and strengthen interdisciplinary connections across the campus.
          </p>
          <p>
           Students may choose to take Italian language courses and/or courses in Italian literature, film, history and culture. Students may also choose to pursue a
           <strong>
            <a href="/main/academics/llc/majorsandminors/italianstudiesminor/#d.en.332356">
             minor in Italian Studies
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
           <br />
           <br />
           By studying a foreign language, students are uniquely poised to take on the challenges and opportunities that globalization offers them. They&nbsp;gain the tools to understand, appreciate and critically analyze cultures other than their own.
           <strong>
            Italian language
           </strong>
           courses offer students the opportunity to acquire or advance communication skills in Italian, with a focus on contemporary Italian&nbsp;culture and society. Studying Italian at Muhlenberg not only gives students the means to communicate with Italians and directly experience life in Italy, but it also allows them to&nbsp;develop an understanding of the treasures of Italian cultural and artistic production, stretching back to Dante's
           <em>
            Divine Comedy.
           </em>
           <br />
           <br />
           <strong>
            Italian Studies
           </strong>
           courses explore topics ranging from the history of Italian cuisine to the Holocaust in Italian literature &amp; film, ensuring that students are exposed to a wide range of Italian literature, art, history and culture. These courses are offered in English and Italian by professors from across the campus, schooled in various academic disciplines &ndash; Italian studies, English, history, political science, art history, theater &ndash; affording students various entryways and points of view on Italian culture.
           <br />
           <br />
           Many Muhlenberg students choose to
           <strong>
            study abroad
           </strong>
           for a summer, a semester or a year, and Italy is their most popular non-English speaking destination. Muhlenberg students attend an array of programs in Italy, depending on their interests and areas of study. These programs include the
           <a href="http://www.dell-arte.org/">
            Accademia dell&rsquo;Arte in Arezzo
           </a>
           , for students of theater, music and dance; the
           <a href="http://www.sienaitalianstudies.com/">
            Siena Italian Studies
           </a>
           , for students wishing to immerse themselves deeply&nbsp;in Italian culture and society; the
           <a href="http://www.fua.it/">
            Florence University of the Arts
           </a>
           , for a vast array of courses and learning opportunities; and other programs around Italy.
          </p>
