         <div class="content-add10">
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: small;">
            What is a GIS?
           </span>
           <br />
           <em>
            <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
             A geographic information system (GIS) is a tool used to map and analyze objects.&nbsp; It assembles, stores, manipulates and displays geographically referenced information.
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: small;">
            How does it work?
           </span>
           <br />
           <em>
            <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
             A GIS retrieves maps and other data which are then stored or filed as layers of information.
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: small;">
            Why use it?
           </span>
           <br />
           <em>
            <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
             GIS technology enables complex analyses to be made about the spatial properties and potential relationships of objects and events.&nbsp; It integrates the benefits offered by both database operations and those offered by the visual perspective of maps.
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
