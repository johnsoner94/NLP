           <h1>
            How does Jose Flores '16 connect with local Allentown adults?
           </h1>
           <p>
            On Thursday mornings during the school year, Jose Flores travels to St. Luke's Evangelical Lutheran Church on North Seventh Street. For the past two years, he&rsquo;s worked in the Adult English as a Second Language (ESL) Conversation Group program. The group helps Lehigh Valley residents improve their command of the English language.
           </p>
           <p>
            Classes consist of adults who have called the Lehigh Valley home for more than a decade as well as newer residents who have only just recently arrived. The group helps adults learn the conversational skills they need on a daily basis.
           </p>
           <p>
            Jose &mdash; a
            <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/polisci/">
             political science
            </a>
            major and
            <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/llc/majors_minors/spanish.html">
             Spanish
            </a>
            minor &mdash; first heard about the opportunity through Beth Halpern, director of Muhlenberg&rsquo;s office of
            <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-service/">
             Community Engagement
            </a>
            , and he felt an immediate connection to the program. He grew up in a home where his parents spoke only Spanish, while his Brooklyn neighbors primarily spoke Hebrew and Yiddish. Jose&rsquo;s first formal experience with the English language came via elementary school classroom discussions.
           </p>
           <p>
            Adult learners, however, often encounter greater difficulties than children when adopting a second language. St. Luke&rsquo;s Conversation Group supports these adults through a community environment where they can meet and communicate with other students like themselves.
           </p>
           <p>
            &ldquo;When I heard about working with an adult ESL program, I just jumped on it,&rdquo; says Jose. &ldquo;I had the same need when I was a child, even though I was born here in the United States. If the help I received in school is something I&rsquo;m able to provide to an adult learner, then that&rsquo;s a program I completely support.&rdquo;
           </p>
           <p>
            Jose admits that his fluency of both English and Spanish has served him well in the adult ESL program, but he&rsquo;s quick to point out that command of the Spanish language isn&rsquo;t required or even expected of volunteers.
           </p>
           <p>
            &ldquo;For some reason, when people hear &lsquo;ESL,&rsquo; it&rsquo;s assumed that Spanish is the student&rsquo;s first language &mdash; but that&rsquo;s not always the case. The ability to converse and explain words and phrases in English is what ESL students need, says Jose. &ldquo;We&rsquo;re helping people learn English; it&rsquo;s not about us learning Spanish or other languages.&rdquo;
           </p>
           <p>
            Jose has encouraged friends to join him on his weekly visits, and has also been joined by students from Dr.
            <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/biology/faculty/niesenbaum.html">
             Rich Niesenbaum
            </a>
            &rsquo;s
            <a href="http://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/global-education/short_term_abroad/">
             Costa Rica MILA program
            </a>
            . As part of a service-learning class, those students are required to work with Allentown communities before volunteering abroad. Many find that after a few volunteer sessions, they enjoy the experience so much that they stay long past their required hours are complete.
           </p>
           <p>
            &ldquo;You see the faces of people who don&rsquo;t know what you&rsquo;re talking about; as weeks progress, though, you start talking to those students more, and they start talking back to you,&rdquo; says Jose. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s an incredible experience.&rdquo;
           </p>
