     <section id="module-position-PiWkyw8jPgY" class="storytopbar-bucket story-headline-module story-story-headline-module">
      <h1 class="asset-headline" itemprop="headline">
       Beth Garrison provides backbone for Muhlenberg volleyball
      </h1>
     </section>
     <section id="module-position-PiWkyw8-F6c" class="storytopbar-bucket story-byline-module story-story-byline-module">
      <div class="asset-metabar">
       <span class="asset-metabar-author asset-metabar-item">
        Bill Arsenault, For the York Daily Record
       </span>
       <span class="asset-metabar-time asset-metabar-item nobyline">
        12:16 a.m. EDT September 20, 2016
       </span>
      </div>
     </section>
     <section id="module-position-PiWkyw9uQNE" class="storytopbar-bucket piano-module story-piano-module">
     </section>
     <section id="module-position-PiWkyw8wllQ" class="storytopbar-bucket google-survey-module story-google-survey-module">
     </section>
     <div class="asset-double-wide double-wide p402_premium" role="main" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div id="module-position-PiWky2CCn30" class="story-asset story-metadata-asset">
       <div class="article-metadata-wrap">
        <section id="module-position-PiWky2CNXnE" class="storymetadata-bucket expandable-photo-module story-expandable-photo-module">
         <aside itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" class="single-photo expandable-collapsed">
          <div class="image-wrap">
           <img class="expand-img-horiz" itemprop="url" src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/157f9c23893b65dd63ca3b2f4c58b5ca89df4448/c=0-0-1117-840&amp;r=x404&amp;c=534x401/local/-/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099033449861757-Elizabeth-Garrison.jpg" alt="636099033449861757-Elizabeth-Garrison.jpg" data-mycapture-src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099033449861757-Elizabeth-Garrison.jpg" data-mycapture-sm-src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/2013a592abfc062045ded36eb81669cc3c0f8592/r=500x350/local/-/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099033449861757-Elizabeth-Garrison.jpg" />
           <span class="toggle">
           </span>
           <meta itemprop="name" content="636099033449861757-Elizabeth-Garrison.jpg" />
          </div>
         </aside>
        </section>
        <p class="image-credit-wrap">
         <span class="js-caption-wrapper">
          <span class="cutline js-caption">
           In her fourth year with Muhlenberg volleyball, libero Beth Garrison has over close to 1,100 career digs and 130 assists.
          </span>
          <meta itemprop="copyrightHolder" content="" />
          <span class="credit">
           (Photo: Submitted)
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The hitters and blockers get most of the oohs and aahs in a volleyball match. But, with that being said, the servers, diggers and passers are the blue collar workers in the sport.
      </p>
      <p>
       And, Beth Garrison is one of the best in Division III when it comes to the dirty work.
      </p>
      <p>
       The senior from Glen Rock and a Susquehannock High School graduate is the backbone of the Muhlenberg women’s volleyball team.
      </p>
      <p>
       “Without good digging, serving and passing, hitters would never have an opportunity to get kills,” she said. “I think the hitters are really appreciative of how important my job is, so whenever I have a great dig that gives them another opportunity, they are quick to acknowledge it and get excited. To me, getting a good dig or ace is about as exciting as it can get.”
      </p>
      <p>
       If that is the case, the 5-foot-5 libero has had a lot of excitement in her four years with the Mules. She currently has 1,096 digs, 130 service aces and 130 assists, the latter despite the fact that she’s not the team’s setter. This season, she has 191 digs, 37 assists and 18 service aces to help Muhlenberg start the season with an 8-3 record after 3-0 victories over McDaniel and Kean last Saturday in Allentown. Garrison had 17 digs in each match.
      </p>
      <div id="module-position-PiWky2CYgcY" class="story-asset image-asset">
       <aside itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" class="single-photo float">
        <img itemprop="url" src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/1c2dfb1cdb3e1d1be344dde516444560382132a7/c=0-17-827-1120&amp;r=183&amp;c=0-0-180-240/local/-/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099034189779243-Elizabeth-Garrison-mugshot.jpg" alt="Elizabeth Garrison" width="180" height="240" data-mycapture-src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099034189779243-Elizabeth-Garrison-mugshot.jpg" data-mycapture-sm-src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/a89a380e747da756a3ac9ad229d86e754bd51ad8/r=290x400/local/-/media/2016/09/19/PAGroup/YorkDailyRecord/636099034189779243-Elizabeth-Garrison-mugshot.jpg" />
        <meta itemprop="name" content="636099034189779243-Elizabeth-Garrison-mugshot.jpg" />
        <meta itemprop="height" content="240" />
        <meta itemprop="width" content="180" />
       </aside>
       <p>
        Elizabeth Garrison
        <meta itemprop="copyrightHolder" content="Submitted" />
        &nbsp;
        <span class="credit">
         (Photo: Submitted)
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Veteran coach Alexa Keckler has benefited from Garrison’s play, as the team holds a 74-32 record when Garrison’s been on the court.
      </p>
      <p>
       “We recruited Beth, but she has surpassed our expectations as a defender,” Keckler said. “Her work ethic is untouchable and has proven to aid her in becoming a fantastic Libero.”
      </p>
      <p>
       Keckler agrees that Garrison’s job isn’t glamorous except maybe in the players' eyes, but she also knows the importance of what she does – especially in the serving department.
      </p>
      <p>
       “Scoring points from the service line is one of the only controllable things in the game of volleyball,” the coach said. "Beth has figured out tactically how to attack weaker passers by serving different routes which has ultimately made her a fantastic server for our program.”
      </p>
      <p>
       And then there’s the defense which is definitely not a glamour job.
      </p>
      <p>
       “Beth loves to play defense,” Keckler said. “I would bet she’d call it glamorous. What she does is not easy, but she lives for preventing opponents from scoring on their attack.”
      </p>
      <p>
       And Garrison feels that she’s getting better.
      </p>
      <p>
       “Each year I feel as though I’m gaining more insight and knowledge about the game,” she said. “I’ve gotten better at picking up hitter tendencies and reading where I think the ball is going to go.”
      </p>
      <p>
       That makes Garrison the “go-to” player when the Mules get stuck in difficult situations. It also helps that she’s one of the team captains.
      </p>
      <p>
       “I have really improved my ability to motivate the team on and off the court and have them perform to their best athletically and academically,” she said.
      </p>
      <p>
       It’s been a great career for Garrison, but one thing is missing: Muhlenberg hasn’t captured a Centennial Conference title and earned a trip to the NCAA Division III Tournament during her tenure.
      </p>
      <p>
       “Both are definitely a personal and a team goal,” Garrison said. “We’ve made it to the conference finals twice (freshman and sophomore years) since I’ve been here, so it would be nice to finally come out on top. We certainly have the talent and dedication to get there this season.”
      </p>
      <p>
       Unfortunately, Garrison is the only senior seeing action with the Mules.
      </p>
      <p>
       “We are young but that only means our roof is a lot higher, so we can surprise a lot of teams,” Keckler said.
      </p>
      <p>
       A chemistry major with a business administration minor, Garrison will graduate in May with honors. She made the Dean’s List six semesters thus far.
      </p>
      <p>
       “After graduation, I plan on attending dental school,” she said. “I hope to achieve my doctorate degree in dentistry and eventually have my own practice.”
      </p>
      <p>
       If she shows the same determination that she has on the volleyball court, she can order her shingle any time she wants.
      </p>
