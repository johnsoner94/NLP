           <p>
            <strong>
             Research Interests
             <br />
             <br />
            </strong>
            My lab studies the molecular genetics of development in the nematode
            <em>
             <span class="italic">
              Caenorhabditis elegans
             </span>
            </em>
            . This microscopic worm has proved a fruitful model for understanding basic biological problems. Research students in my lab are studying a gene called
            <em>
             <span class="italic">
              fax-1
             </span>
            </em>
            , which functions in development of neurons and a related gene called
            <em>
             <span class="italic">
              nhr-67
             </span>
            </em>
            , which functions in the development of various cells and organs. These studies provide models for understanding an inherited form of human blindness called Enhanced S-Cone Sensitivity and for understanding the mechanism cell-to-cell signaling and cancer. For more detailed information, consult the
            <a href="/main/academics/biology/facultystaff/brucewightman/drwightmanspage/wightmanlabpage/">
             Wightman Research Lab homepage
            </a>
            .
