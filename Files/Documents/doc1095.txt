           <h3>
            Muhlenberg College Air Conditioner Policy
           </h3>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Purpose
            </strong>
            <br />
            The purpose of this policy is to provide information for the Muhlenberg College community on the requirements for eligibility for air conditioning in student housing, the guidelines for window units and the process for having a unit installed.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             Policy
            </strong>
            <br />
            A request for air conditioning with appropriate documentation must be submitted to Student Health Services (Fax #484-664-3522). All requests will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis and documentation of a need for air conditioning does not guarantee that your application will be approved. Also, there is no guarantee that you will be housed in an area equipped with central air. If you select to live in a residence hall without central air, you need to provide your own window unit.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             Requirements and Process
            </strong>
            <br />
            Documentation from the physician providing care is required indicating a need for air conditioning. The student or the student's physician must provide documentation to the Director of Student Health Services (Fax #484-664-3522). Approvals are determined on a case by case basis by the Director of Student Health Services, and are dependent upon the documentation received relative to the severity of illness and the availability of rooms that are centrally air conditioned or have the capability of supporting a window AC unit.
            <br />
            <br />
            The documentation must include the diagnosis, treatment plan, and any additional information that would support the need for this intervention. Physician notes on a prescription such as "Please provide an air conditioner for my patient due to allergies" will not be accepted as documenting the need. Priority will be given to those students who have provided documented evidence of exacerbation of chronic respiratory diseases including asthma, chronic medical conditions, and those receiving allergy injections on a regular basis. Documentation needs to be up dated each year to maintain eligibility.&nbsp;
            <strong>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             All request must be received by June 5th for the following academic year.
            </strong>
            <br />
            <br />
            The College uses ADA standards to determine pre-lottery housing. Simply needing air conditioning is not a reason to be housed before the campus body.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             Residence Halls with Central Air
            </strong>
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Benfer Hall
            </span>
            - 8 person suite styled building, once heat is on AC does not work, suites need to be filled in order to live in this building, houses upper-class students
            <br />
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Taylor Hall
            </span>
            - double rooms with heat and AC systems that work independently, houses upper-class students
            <br />
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Robertson and South Halls
            </span>
            - 4 person suite styled building, systems work independently, suites need to be filled in order to live in these buildings, A/C outdoor unit does not work when outdoor air temperature is 47F or lower, houses upper-class students
            <br />
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             The Village
            </span>
            - 4 and 5 person suite styled building, systems work independently, suites need to be filled in order to live in these buildings, houses upper-class students
            <br />
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Walz Hall
            </span>
            - double rooms, once heat is on AC does not work on first 3 floors, houses first year students
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="italic">
             2201 Chew&nbsp;St -
            </span>
            4 person suite styled building, systems work independently, suites need to be filled in order to live in these buildings, houses upper-class students
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Prosser Hall Ground Annex.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             East Hall
            </em>
            <em>
             -
            </em>
            double,single and triple rooms with heat and AC systems that work independently.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Residence Halls where window units can be installed
            </strong>
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Brown Hall
            </span>
            <br />
            <br />
            <span class="italic">
             Martin Luther Hall
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Prosser Hall
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Window Unit Requirements:
            </strong>
            <br />
            No higher than 14"
            <br />
            No more than 120 volts
            <br />
            No more than 7000 BTUs
            <br />
            Must be
            <a href="http://www.energystar.gov/index.cfm?c=home.index" target="_blank">
             Energy Star
            </a>
            rated (Read the
            <a href="/media/contentassets/pdf/campuslife/resserv/pgcstatement.pdf">
             President's Greening Committee Statement
            </a>
            regarding this requirement)
            <br />
            <br />
            Need extension cord that is 12-3 wire with a ground for A/C units. The College does not provide extension cords.
            <br />
            <br />
            <strong>
             Installation Information
            </strong>
            <br />
            In order to ensure window units are installed correctly, the Plant Operations staff must install them. Once you have been approved by the Health Center, you will receive an email reminding you of what needs to occur. Requests approved once the school year has started will normally be installed within a 3 business day period.
           </p>
