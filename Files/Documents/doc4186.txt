       <div class="text-post-wrap">
        <h2>
         <a href="http://muhlenbergtheatredance.tumblr.com/post/132098842236/fall-dance-gallery-first-showing" title="Fall Dance Gallery- First Showing">
          Fall Dance Gallery- First Showing
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         One showing down,
one to go! This past weekend marked the first showing for
         <i>
          Fall Dance Gallery
         </i>
         and it is crazy to me that we are already at
this point in the rehearsal process. It feels like we began so recently, but already,
both of the pieces I am in for the concert are well underway. I always love to
see what everyone has been working on at the showings and it reminds me how lucky
I am to be able to work alongside such creative minds and bodies, specifically
Cat Chythlook and Emma Bownes, my choreographers this year. While it is fun to
dance in my friends&rsquo; pieces, getting to know someone as a choreographer first
is a very special experience, which is how it is with the pieces I am in this
year. It is very interesting to get to know how someone works creatively and
how this creative process is influenced by the nuances of their personality.
        </p>
        <p>
         Emma&rsquo;s piece is unlike
any dance I have ever been a part of before. One of the very first things she
said to us in rehearsal was &ldquo;This piece is going to be really weird,&rdquo; but she
clarified that, of course, it would be a &ldquo;good weird&rdquo;. She has definitely
delivered on those promises.
        </p>
        <p>
         The piece is about waking up in the morning. There
are ten dancers in the piece, so she was able to create a really cool Rube
Goldberg machine-type chain reaction image; each person&rsquo;s movements cause
another person to &ldquo;wake up.&rdquo; Since there are so many of us, there&rsquo;s always a
lot going on, almost chaotically, so the audience will have a lot to look at.
Another &ldquo;good weird&rdquo; thing about this rehearsal process is that almost every
week, Emma throws bizarre positions or ways of travelling at us. She tells us
that she&rsquo;ll just be goofing around in her room and accidently fall into a
certain position that somehow really works, which causes her to have a light
bulb moment and say &ldquo;I have to put this in my piece!&rdquo; &nbsp;In this way, we incorporated the products of
her playing around in her room. This included, but was not limited to, a crazy
yoga-like
         <a>
         </a>
         position wherein you put your weight on your
hands and face with your legs up in the air, and also a way of moving in a
squatted position while shifting on one hand so that your body, in its balled
up squat position, scoots through the air. Gotta love her.
        </p>
        <p>
         Aside from the
zany positions she has us contorting our bodies into, it has been a pleasure so
far to work with Emma and the nine other dancers in the piece, and I can&rsquo;t wait
to see what happens in the coming weeks regarding
         <i>
          Dance Gallery
         </i>
         rehearsals!
        </p>

      <div class="article-content">

       <div class="text-post-wrap">
        <h2>
         <a href="http://muhlenbergtheatredance.tumblr.com/post/132091200061/adaptation-accomplished" title="Adaptation: Accomplished">
          Adaptation: Accomplished
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; Exciting news from my production of Kurt Vonnegut, Jr.&rsquo;s
&ldquo;2 B R 0 2 B&rdquo;: after two great weeks of rehearsal,
we finally have a script!
        </p>
        <p>
         &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;That statement
might sound backward for your average piece of theatre, where a script is
probably the starting point. But &ldquo;2 B R 0 2 B&rdquo;
is anything but average. The production, which I am adapting and
directing for this fall&rsquo;s Red Door Play Festival, started with
Vonnegut&rsquo;s short story instead. Then, we added a director (yours
truly), a stage manager, a cast, and people to take care of some of the
trickier set and sound elements. The script, in many ways, came last.
        </p>
        <p>
         &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Instead, we
began rehearsals with a read-through of Vonnegut&rsquo;s short story. The
characters read their dialogue, and the four-person chorus traded off the lines
of narration. Then, we reduced the script to just the character&rsquo;s
lines and began blocking their action&mdash; which I have come to call the &ldquo;core&rdquo;
narrative. As the parts of the cast playing named characters got used to
their own arcs within the story and explored character work, the chorus met
with me to decide exactly how the remaining lines of Vonnegut&rsquo;s
story would be divided. We decided whether text would be best represented out
loud as a line spoken by the chorus or implicitly through something in either
the physicality of the actors or some technical element. This process was long;
it took two full rehearsals to decide what would be said aloud and how it would
be split up, and then I took the selections and assigned them to the various
chorus members.
        </p>
        <p>
         &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;This past
Saturday, we had our first read-through of the script in its full iteration. I
don&rsquo;t want to give too much away quite yet, but I can say that I
was extremely proud of my whole creative team for the work they have been doing
to this point. As we began blocking in the rehearsals since then, I can really
see the show coming together. And most beautiful of all: I can see them owning
it. Everyone in the cast has had a hand in originating their role; they are, quite literally,
the owners of this material, and I look forward to seeing them share their
passionate, interested work with audiences in three (ahh! just three!) short
weeks.
        </p>

We wear many hats here.
        </p>

         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         We have been in rehearsals for Chicago for quite some time now, and tonight is the night of our first full run-through! It is so exciting to get this show on its feet, finally, after all of the extraordinary work put in by our talented cast members in rehearsal! This process is very different than my show experience last year. During last year&rsquo;s production of Anyone Can Whistle, I was a recent transfer student. I remember not knowing many people, but through the process, getting to know so many wonderful people. This year, I was so ready to have a great experience, yet again, upon being cast in Chicago! The most excitement for me was centered on getting the opportunity to become more experienced with Fosse-style dancing. I have taken classes in the past, but there are not many opportunities to actually work on a show in this style. I was very excited about working with the team of directors: Charlie Richter, Michael Schnack, and Jeffrey Peterson. Though I have worked with Charlie and Michael previously, in Street Scene (where I was also a blogger!) and over the summer in Hello, Dolly!, I was particularly ready to jump into a new style of theater with the team. I was so excited to work with Jeffrey for the first time. &nbsp;I guarantee that audiences will be in awe of his intricate choreography, incorporating Fosse-style dancing with some of his own style! &nbsp;It is fun to work with such an experimental choreographer; Jeffrey has a unique ability to look at his pool of dancers and mold, craft and fit the dancing to best suit his cast. I believe that the dancing will be one of the most memorable aspects of Chicago. I can&rsquo;t wait to share how our first full run goes!&nbsp;

         Atlas
        </i>
        was a success. This weekend was finally show time and it was
an experience that I will never forget. Located in Baker Theater in the Trexler
Pavilion for Theatre &amp; Dance,
        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">
         Atlas
        </i>
        sold out all three shows: Friday night, Saturday afternoon, and Saturday night.
All three performances even had wait-lists of students who wanted to see the
show, some for a second time around. Loosely based on the story of
        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">
         Alice in Wonderland
        </i>
        with acrobatics,
aerial stunts, Cyr wheel, juggling, dancing, and comedy,
        <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:
normal">
         Atlas
        </i>
        became the talk of the town.
        <br />
        <br />
        I have never been so proud of the
final results of a show. Each individual act was extremely entertaining and
awe-inspiring given the impossible feats the cast performed. But it wasn&rsquo;t just
the raw talent that made this show what it was. Everything from the intricately
planned out stage design to the literal hundreds of light cues brought this
show to a new level &mdash; not to mention the unparalleled atmosphere of this cast
that has worked tirelessly throughout the year to the point where we have
become a family.
        <br />
        <br />
        To think that only five months ago
we had no idea if we would get to perform on a stage. Now we have finished a
spectacularly successful weekend. I have never felt such a rush of pride as the
moment when the curtain rose for bows on opening night and the audience was
already on their feet cheering.
        <span style="mso-spacerun:yes">
         &nbsp;
        </span>
        It was
only after the curtain went down after our final show that it truly hit me that
this beautiful creation I was a part of had brought people to tears (audience
and cast members alike). Though Circus is finished for this school year I
cannot wait to see what bigger and better things will take place in years to
come

        <h2>
         <a href="http://muhlenbergtheatredance.tumblr.com/post/114424382986/spitzprobe-erica" title="Spitzprobe! - Erica">
          Spitzprobe! - Erica
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Tonight (3/19) the children were called to go over the &ldquo;Children&rsquo;s Game&rdquo; to start off the exciting sitzprobe! This is the German term for a seated rehearsal in which the cast sings with the orchestra for the first time. The orchestra sounds absolutely amazing! Even the warm-up exercises that the orchestra played while the cast was settling into their seats was exciting. I believe the cast knows this show backwards and forwards by now, because we have been doing run-throughs for SO long. An exciting sitzprobe was exactly what our cast needed to jump-start tech week. The music sounded fresh, exciting, and new again. Street Scene&rsquo;s score is so beautiful, and I can&rsquo;t wait to share it with an audience! &nbsp;I was surprised that I have never heard any of this gorgeous music before.
        </p>
        <p>
         The sitzprobe began with all of the children&rsquo;s music, which at times can get tricky because we often sing from off-stage, so the musical director, Michael Schnack, cannot always conduct us. That&rsquo;s where our fabulous child wrangler, Annie, goes into action. She will be conducting the children off-stage. After going over these tricky spots, the ensemble came onstage to start working out the first of many group numbers. With the addition of the orchestra, the stakes were raised and the cast got a fresh perspective on the music that encompassed so much more emotional depth than we&rsquo;ve been accustomed to.
        </p>
        <p>
         Also, I had a final costume fitting today! My costume was changed from an adorable outfit I had tried on at my last fitting; however, I enjoy the new one even more! I cannot wait to wear it in the show. Street Scene begins tech rehearsals tonight, and I am so excited to get the ball rolling toward next week&rsquo;s opening!
        </p>
        <p>
         While I was sitting with the children during the sitzprobe, one of them did happen to provide me with a little chuckle in the middle of such a heightened rehearsal. She said to me, &ldquo;I&rsquo;m so tiny! How am I supposed to sing louder in this big place?!&rdquo; To which I quoted in my head the famous line from Gypsy: &ldquo;Sing out, Louise!&rdquo;
        </p>

I could barely believe it too when our director Noah Dach &lsquo;16, told us our final full cast rehearsal before spring break would involve a trip to Muhlenberg&rsquo;s indoor pool. All the faculty that viewed our presentation felt we were on the right track, which was a huge compliment to us all. But that doesn&rsquo;t mean all the hard work is over. Noah told us in our first rehearsal after the break that these next three weeks would be &ldquo;the most important of them all.&ldquo; No pressure right? Luckily this talented cast can handle whatever is thrown at them. This is the time when the scenes of acrobatics, dancing, aerial silks, juggling, and clowning all come together to make a cohesive story.

        <h2>
         <a href="http://muhlenbergtheatredance.tumblr.com/post/112149509436/designer-run-erica" title="Designer Run - Erica">
          Designer Run - Erica
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Tonight&rsquo;s run was interesting to say the very least! Annie (our fabulous child wrangler) was not present for rehearsal and I had to act as pseudo-wrangler. &ldquo;Being&rdquo; a child and &ldquo;wrangling&rdquo; the children are TWO SERPARATE JOBS. Let that statement be known. I didn&rsquo;t have to do the dirty work of actually conducting the children, as Annie does, but I did have to make sure they were well-behaved and knew when to go on and off of the stage. It was convenient for me, because those are my entrances and exits as well. As expected, I did have an easy time working as the child&rsquo;s superior (despite most of them being my height), although some of them did not take me totally seriously because, after all, we are all &ldquo;children&rdquo; in Street Scene. However, it was fun to get to take on another fun job at rehearsal for a day!
        </p>
        <p>
         The run as a whole went very successfully! It feels great showing off our production to the professionals who have been working on the show behind the scenes. All aspects of the production seem to be going according to plan, and making incredible progress as my cast mates and I become more and more familiar with the work. It is also a privilege to get to see the professional vocal teachers (at Muhlenberg) Lauren and Ed perform each day. For those of you who don&rsquo;t know, Lauren Curnow and Ed Bara, faculty members, are playing two of the lead roles in the production. Working with them, and simply observing them as they work has been inspiring and highly educational.
         <br />
        </p>
        <p>
         As usual, I will end my blog post with a favorite quote stated by a child cast member. &ldquo;I will ALWAYS be thought of as the cutest one in a group because&hellip; well&hellip; JUST LOOK AT ME! I&rsquo;M SHORT!&rdquo; &hellip; Afterwards I just nodded and agreed, &ldquo;Me too&hellip;. Me too&rdquo;.
         <br />
        </p>

     <article id="111377830906" class="type_text muhlenberg_college theatre_dance dance_emerge loren scrollable" rel="https://www.tumblr.com/reblog/111377830906/ak5PqbHD">

       <div class="text-post-wrap">
        <h2>
         <a href="http://muhlenbergtheatredance.tumblr.com/post/111377830906/dance-emerge-begins-loren" title="Dance Emerge Begins! - Loren">
          Dance Emerge Begins! - Loren
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="MsoNormal">
         Wow! What a whirlwind semester this has been so far, and
we&rsquo;re only five weeks in! Ok, so let me tell you what&rsquo;s been going on so far in
the Dance Department. The first week of every semester consists of auditions,
so in the fall, there are auditions for
         <i>
          Master
Choreographers, Moving Stories, and Fall Gallery
         </i>
         , and in the spring there
are auditions for
         <i>
          Dance Emerge
         </i>
         and
         <i>
          Spring Gallery
         </i>
         . Also at the start of the
spring semester, rehearsals for
         <i>
          Master
Choreographers
         </i>
         pick right back up because that concert performs during the first
weekend of February! I was lucky enough to be cast in a Masters piece this year,
and those performances were definitely one of the highlights of my year. My
choreographer was Jeffrey Peterson, a faculty member here at Muhlenberg. The
piece was based upon the notion of fight or flight, so the movement was very
weighted and grounded. There are some pictures on the Theatre and Dance website
(
         <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fbit.ly%2F18Oetru&amp;t=ZWIyZGMxY2YwODYwZTc4MTdiYWQ4NDY5ZTk1ZTcwOGZiYWQ4MGMyNCxhazVQcWJIRA%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3ALq3WNlBnejLbuPHncgjosA&amp;m=1" target="_blank">
          bit.ly/18Oetru
         </a>
         ) that you can check out!
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal">
         But back to that first week. In order to choreograph for
         <i>
          Moving Stories
         </i>
         or
         <i>
          Dance Emerge
         </i>
         , each student has to propose choreography, an idea,
and music in front of a group of faculty, and then they choose who will get to
choreograph throughout the semester. I have previously choreographed for two
Gallery Concerts, the first last spring, and the second last fall. Because of
my past choreographic experience, the fact that I am enrolled in Dance
Composition class, as well as having been an understudy in this past year&rsquo;s
         <i>
          Moving Stories
         </i>
         concert, I was eligible to
propose for
         <i>
          Dance Emerge
         </i>
         .&nbsp; So, I decided to give it a shot. I felt it was a
good decision, because even if I didn&rsquo;t get chosen, at least the faculty would
know that I was serious, and then hopefully consider me for future concerts.
Well, the proposal meeting was on Monday and the email came out on Thursday with
my name on the list!! This was beyond exciting because
now I would get costumes and lighting designed by the department production
team, which are not provided for the Gallery concerts. Tomorrow we already have
our first
         <i>
          Dance Emerge
         </i>
         showing in
front of the artistic director, Jeffrey, as well as the lighting and costume
crew. I&rsquo;ll be back with an update after the showing!

         </a>
        </p>
