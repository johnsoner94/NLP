          <h1>
          </h1>
          <div class="content-add05">
           <h3>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Past Exhibitions
            </span>
           </h3>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <strong>
             What's the good of Mercator's North Poles and Equators?
            </strong>
            <br />
            Curated by Alastair Noble
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <span class="mceNonEditable">
             <img alt="homepage image" src="/terminalfour/SiteManager?ctfn=download&amp;fnno=60&amp;ceid=190233821" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" />
             <span style="font-size: 70%;">
              Drift 1994, 17 min, colour, sound, 16mm [still] Chris Welsby
             </span>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <span class="style2">
             January 20 &ndash; February 20, 2016
             <br />
             Artist Talk: January 27 4:30pm in the Martin Art Gallery
             <br />
             Opening Reception: Wednesday January 20 4:30 &ndash; 6:30pm
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <span class="style2">
             <br />
             The Martin Art Gallery at Muhlenberg College is pleased to present
             <strong>
              <em>
               What's the good of Mercator's North Poles and Equators?
              </em>
             </strong>
             Curated by Alastair Noble. This multidisciplinary exhibition be on view January 20 through February 20, 2016 with a public reception occurring Wednesday, January 20, from 4:30 &ndash; 6:00 pm. Additionally, the public is invited to join us in the gallery for a talk featuring the curator, on Wednesday January 27, from 4:30 &ndash; 5:30pm.
             <br />
             <br />
             This exhibition presents six artists that are engaged in mapping through intensive research of either the land or sea or both but without conforming to any &ldquo;conventional signs&rdquo; other than those they have invented. In this case the artist&rsquo;s tabula rasa transforms into a personal meditation on place, evoking both palpable and mythological sites presented through varied media.
             <br />
             <br />
             Works on display include US and international artists David Faithfull, Cathy Lane, Paul Martin, Brian Nissen, James Walsh and Chris Welsby, and the exhibition will be accompanied by a catalog and essay by the Curator.
            </span>
           </p>
